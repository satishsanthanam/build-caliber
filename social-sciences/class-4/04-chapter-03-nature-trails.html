Class 4 — Chapter 3: Nature Trail

3.1 The Adventure Begins

The students, along with their teacher, embark on a nature trail at Pachmarhi in Madhya Pradesh. Guided by Abha Didi, a nature scientist, they explore the forest while learning to observe wildlife without causing harm.

Safety Rules: DOs and DON'Ts in a Forest

  • Be careful in a new place.
  • Do not tease or feed animals.
  • Do not damage trees and flowers.
  • Do not bring pets, firearms, weapons, or polythene bags.
  • Do not damage public utilities.
  • Do not throw garbage in the forest.
3.2 Animals of the Forest

Forests are home to a diverse range of land animals, birds, and insects, each possessing unique physical features and adaptations.

Unique Forest Animals

  • Indian Giant Squirrel: A large, red-coloured squirrel native to Pachmarhi and select other regions.
  • Spectacled Monkey: The state animal of Tripura, recognized by the white patches around its eyes that resemble spectacles.
  • Gaur: Also known as the Indian Bison, they resemble strong buffaloes but have shorter horns.

Animal Footprints

Footprints in the mud help identify which animals visit water bodies to drink. Observing and drawing footprints is a key method used by nature scientists to track wildlife.

3.3 Birds, Beaks, and Claws

Birds exhibit distinct physical adaptations based on their habitats and feeding habits.

Key Bird Features

  • Hornbill: Characterized by a unique horn-like headgear growing out of its forehead.
  • Peacock: Known for its colorful plumage.
  • Owl: Equipped with sharp night vision to hunt in the dark.

Beak and Claw Adaptations

  • Eagle: Has a sharp, curved beak and strong, sharp claws to catch and tear prey.
  • Sunbird: Features a long, slender beak designed to drink nectar from flowers.
  • Sparrow: Possesses a short, strong beak ideal for cracking seeds.
3.4 Water Animals

Animals living in and around water bodies have specialized features that facilitate survival and movement.

  • Fish: Use fins to swim.
  • Gharial: A type of crocodile with a long, narrow, and pointed snout adapted for catching fish.
  • Turtles: Protected by strong shells and equipped with webbed feet for swimming.
  • Frogs: Adapted to live both on land and in water.
3.5 Tiny Animals (Insects)

Insects represent a highly diverse group of tiny animals found throughout forest ecosystems.

Characteristics of Insects

  • Possess three pairs of legs (six legs in total).
  • Have one pair of antennae to sense their surroundings.
  • Often have two pairs of wings (e.g., flies, grasshoppers, butterflies).
  • Common examples include ants, beetles, bees, flies, praying mantises, and butterflies.
3.6 Plants and Leaves

Plants can be identified by the unique characteristics of their leaves, including color, shape, texture, and venation.

Leaf Veins

The lines visible on a leaf are called veins. Different plants exhibit distinct patterns of vein arrangement, which can be studied using the "Leaf Autograph" crayon-rubbing technique.

3.7 The Web of Life

All living beings and natural elements are interconnected in an ecosystem.

Interdependence

Plants, animals, water, soil, and the Sun rely on one another. The Web of Life game demonstrates that if one element of nature disappears (such as plants or a specific animal), the entire ecological web weakens or collapses, highlighting the critical need for conservation.
