Class 4 — Chapter 2: Exploring Our Neighbourhood

2.1 On Our Way Back Home: Communication

The journey back from school introduces various elements of our neighbourhood, starting with how people connect and communicate.

The Postal System

The post office is a key public service that helps people stay connected by sending letters, parcels, and documents.

  • Postbox: A red box where letters are dropped.
  • Postman: Collects letters from the postbox and takes them to the post office.
  • Sorting: The post office sorts letters according to their destination regions before delivery.

Evolution of Communication

Communication methods have evolved significantly over time:

  • Ancient Times: People used drumbeats and carvings on rocks.
  • Later: Letters were carried by messengers and pigeons.
  • Postal Era: The establishment of formal postal services for letters and parcels.
  • Inventions: The telephone, radio, and television made sharing information easier.
  • Modern Era: Mobile phones, emails, and the internet allow instant global connection.
2.2 Transportation & Environment

Modern infrastructure helps us travel faster but impacts our natural surroundings.

Modern Transport Infrastructure

  • Metro Trains: High-speed trains that travel very fast, saving time for commuters.
  • Flyovers: High roads built above busy roads and crossings to help vehicles move smoothly and avoid traffic.

Environmental Balance

While modern facilities like flyovers and metros make life easier, they often lead to the loss of trees, birds, and animals. To keep our environment safe and secure, we must create a balance by planting trees.

2.3 Essential Public Services

Neighbourhoods have public places that provide services related to health, safety, cleanliness, and finance.

Healthcare (Hospitals)

A hospital is a crucial community institution where people go when they are unwell.

  • Doctors: Treat patients and prescribe medicines.
  • Nurses: Care for patients and assist doctors.
  • Community Camps: Hospitals often organize health camps, such as eye check-up camps, to serve the public.

Financial Services (Banks & Money)

Banks help individuals manage and secure their finances.

  • Bank: A safe place where money is kept and saved for future needs.
  • ATM (Automated Teller Machine): A machine that allows people to withdraw money at any time.
  • Online Transactions: Modern technology allows instant money transfers using mobile phones.
  • Passbook: A book containing records of bank transactions.

History of Exchange: The Barter System

Before money was invented, people used the barter system to exchange goods and services directly (e.g., exchanging wheat for milk). Money was invented to simplify exchanges because finding the right trade partner and value was difficult.

2.4 Mapping & Directions

Understanding directions is essential for navigating and mapping our neighbourhood.

Finding Directions

Directions can be identified using the rising sun (which rises in the East):

  • North: Shown upwards on a map.
  • South: Shown downwards on a map.
  • East: Shown on the right side of a map.
  • West: Shown on the left side of a map.

Landmarks

Landmarks are easily recognizable structures (like parks, shops, hospitals, or bus stops) that help in giving directions and identifying locations.
