Chapter 10: Our Sky

10.1 Introduction to Our Sky

The sky appears different at various times of the day. Its color changes in the morning, afternoon, and night. Along with the sky, the Sun's color and the Moon's shape and size also appear to change over time. These changes influence the activities of birds, animals, plants, and humans throughout the day.

10.2 The Sun and Its Journey

The Sun is the brightest object in the sky, providing us with light and heat. It is so bright that other stars cannot be seen during the daytime when it is present.

The Sun's Path

The Sun appears to rise in the East in the morning, move overhead till noon, and then gradually move downwards towards the West in the afternoon until it disappears (sets).

10.3 Shadows and Their Behavior

Shadows move with us and change shape based on our poses. The length and direction of shadows change systematically throughout the day:

  • Morning: Shadows are long and point towards the West.
  • Noon: Shadows are at their shortest because the Sun is overhead.
  • Evening: Shadows are long again and point towards the East (opposite to the morning direction).

Key Principles of Shadows

  • If an object is closer to the source of light, its shadow becomes bigger.
  • If the direction of the light source changes, the direction of the shadow also changes.
10.4 Telling Time and Shadow Play

Ancient civilizations used shadows to track time, leading to the invention of the sundial.

Jantar Mantar

Raja Jai Singh of Jaipur built Jantar Mantar observatories in Jaipur, Ujjain, Delhi, Varanasi, and Mathura. These sites feature a massive, highly accurate sundial called the Samrat Yantra, which tells time based on shadow movement.

Shadow Puppetry

Shadow Puppetry is a traditional art form practiced in India for a long time, where cut-outs are used to create stories using light and shadows.

10.5 The Night Sky and the Moon

After sunset, the sky is filled with thousands of twinkling stars that form patterns, such as Saptarshi (Seven Sages). The Moon is the largest and most prominent object in the night sky.

Phases of the Moon

The Moon's shape changes from night to night:

  • Poornima (Full Moon): The night when the Moon is visible as a complete, perfect circle.
  • Amavasya (New Moon): The night when the Moon is not visible at all.
  • The Moon also takes on intermediate shapes, such as a crescent shape resembling the letter 'C'.

Lunar Festivals

Many cultural festivals in India are celebrated based on the phases of the Moon:

  • Diwali: Celebrated on Amavasya (no Moon).
  • Rakshabandhan, Guru Poornima, Buddha Poornima: Celebrated on Poornima (full Moon).
  • Eid: Celebrated on the sighting of the crescent Moon on the first night after a New Moon.
10.6 India's Chandrayaan Mission

India's lunar exploration program, the Chandrayaan Mission, achieved a historic milestone on 23 August 2023, when the Vikram Lander successfully landed on the Moon's surface to collect scientific data.
