Chapter 4: Growing up with Nature

4.1 A Journey Through a Forest

Reena and Amit travel to their village near a forest for a harvest festival. The village is celebrating a special milestone: becoming a fully solar-powered village, where electricity is generated cleanly using solar panels to power appliances like fans and bulbs.

4.2 Welcome to the Village & The Palash Tree

Upon entering the village, the children observe trees covered in vibrant orange-red flowers.

  • The Palash tree is famously known as the "flame of the forest" because when it blooms, the entire forest appears reddish-orange from a distance.
  • In Gujarat, the Palash tree is locally called Kesuda.

Activity 1: Local Flowers

Students are encouraged to identify and list five colorful flowers in their local area along with their local names and drawings.

4.3 Eco-Friendly Traditional Houses

Village houses are constructed differently from city houses, utilizing natural, sustainable materials:

  • Building Materials: Houses are built using clay, hay, cow dung, and other natural resources.
  • Wall Decorations: Beautiful traditional paintings are created on the walls using natural dyes, such as a mixture of rice flour and water.

Activity 2: Model House Making

Create a model of a house using natural materials like clay, wooden sticks, hay, grass, and leaves, and decorate it with a traditional art form.

4.4 Natural Paints and Gond Art

Traditional art relies heavily on colors extracted directly from nature rather than synthetic chemicals.

  • Natural Paint Sources: Paints are prepared using natural extracts of flowers, leaves, and powdered colored stones.
  • Gond art is a traditional form of wall painting that uses natural colors to depict elements of nature, including trees, animals, birds, and humans.

Activity 3: Preparing a Natural Dye

Follow these steps to prepare a natural dye at home:

  1. Step 1: Collect plant parts with coloring properties, such as beetroot, hibiscus, marigold flowers, leaves, barks, or roots.
  2. Step 2: Boil the collected plant parts in approximately $1 ext{--}2$ litres of water for 30 minutes to an hour with a lid on (with adult supervision).
  3. Step 3: Strain the liquid to remove the solid plant materials, leaving behind the pure natural dye.
  4. Application: Soak a light-colored cloth (like a handkerchief) in the dye overnight, squeeze it, and dry it the next morning.
4.5 Traditional Storage and Preservation

Villagers use ingenious, chemical-free methods to store and protect agricultural produce from pests:

  • Earthen Pots: Lined inside with neem leaves to protect stored grains.
  • Bamboo Baskets: Coated with cow dung on the outside to keep insects and pests away.

The Jenu Kurubas Tribe

The Jenu Kurubas is a traditional tribe from Karnataka. Their name is derived from the word 'Jenu', which means honey. When harvesting honey from beehives, they sing songs to apologize to the bees, demonstrating deep respect and gratitude toward nature.

Activity 4: Traditional Preservation Methods

Identify traditional grain and vegetable preservation methods across different regions. For example, in Uttarakhand, a container called Tumri is constructed from a round or oval-shaped dried bottle gourd to store items.

4.6 Natural First-Aid and Medicinal Plants

Nature provides effective remedies for health and protection:

  • Neem oil mixtures can be rubbed on hands and feet as a highly effective, natural mosquito repellent.

Activity 5: Medicinal Plants and Their Uses

Identify common medicinal plants, their useful parts, and applications:

  • Indian Basil (Tulsi): Leaves are used for treating colds, coughs, and boosting immunity.
  • Carom Seeds (Ajwain): Seeds are used to cure stomach aches and aid digestion.
4.7 Celebrating Nature & Sacred Groves

Community celebrations in the village begin with prayers offered to God and Mother Nature, accompanied by the vibrant beats of traditional instruments like the dhol and nagada.

Sacred Groves

Sacred Groves are small patches of forests protected and preserved by local communities. Within these groves, all trees and wildlife are strictly protected. They serve as sacred spaces for communities to gather, worship, and celebrate the beauty of nature.

4.8 Respecting and Protecting Nature

Our rich cultural traditions emphasize respecting and protecting plants and animals through various festivals and daily practices.

Activity 7: Nature-Centric Festivals
  • Vat Purnima: Association with the Banyan tree through worship.
  • Hari Jiroti: Association with fruit-bearing trees through plantation drives.
  • Kaziranga Elephant Festival: Association with elephants to promote conservation awareness.

Actionable Ways to Care for the Environment
  • Reduce paper waste to save trees.
  • Say no to single-use plastics.
  • Spread awareness about saving plants and wildlife.
  • Keep food and fresh water out for birds.
  • Plant local tree species.
  • Organize community clean-up drives.
4.9 Let Us Reflect

Consider the impact of human activities on natural resources:

  • Overusing Wood: Leads to deforestation and habitat loss.
  • Overusing Water: Leads to depletion of groundwater tables and severe droughts.
  • Overusing Soil: Leads to loss of soil fertility and desertification.
  • Overusing Sea Products: Leads to overfishing and disruption of marine ecosystems.
