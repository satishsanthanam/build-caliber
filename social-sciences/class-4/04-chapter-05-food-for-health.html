Class 4 — Chapter 5: Food for Health

Unit 3: Health and Well-being

This unit discusses the important role of balanced food, exercise, and rest for a healthy life. It introduces the diversity of food, the six major tastes (Shada Rasa), the importance of water, and mindful eating habits.

Chapter 5: Food for Health

5.1 Taste Diversity and Mystery Masala

Food brings joy and represents cultural diversity. Different foods offer unique combinations of tastes.

  • Ugadi Pachadi: A traditional food of Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, and Telangana, made with six different taste elements.
  • Shad Rasa: The six tastes recognized in Ayurveda that are essential for a balanced diet:
    • Sweet (meetha) - e.g., Jaggery
    • Sour (khatta) - e.g., Tamarind
    • Salty (namkeen) - e.g., Salt
    • Pungent (teekha) - e.g., Green chilli
    • Bitter (karva) - e.g., Neem
    • Astringent (kasaila) - e.g., Raw mango

5.2 Super Foods (Millets)

Millets are highly nutritious grains rich in essential nutrients.

  • Examples of millets: Jowar (sorghum), Bajra (pearl millet), Ragi (finger millet), and Jau (barley).
  • Traditional dishes include jowar upma, ragi laddu, and bajre ki khichdi.

5.3 Food Groups and Their Functions

Our body requires a variety of food items to stay healthy, energetic, and strong. Foods are classified into three main groups:

  • Energy-giving foods: Rich in carbohydrates and fats. Provide energy to our body. Examples: Rice, potato, wheat, maize, banana, apple, sweet potato, honey, millets, butter, ghee, and oil.
  • Body-building foods: Rich in proteins. Help us grow, build muscles, and make us strong. Examples: Pulses, legumes, soybeans, chickpeas, peanuts, tofu, milk, cheese, beans, almonds, walnuts, eggs, meat, and fish.
  • Protective foods: Rich in vitamins and minerals. Help us fight against and protect us from diseases. Examples: Fruits and vegetables like carrot, orange, lemon, papaya, spinach, cabbage, guava, amla, and tomato.

5.4 Balanced Diet and Junk Food

  • Balanced Food Plate: A plate containing the right quantities of food from all three food groups, with protective foods (fruits and vegetables) making up the largest portion, followed by body-building and energy-providing foods.
  • Junk Food: Foods containing high amounts of oil, salt, and sugar that are unhealthy if consumed regularly. Examples: Soft drinks, chips, bread pakoda, burgers, and pizza. Fresh home-cooked food is always preferred over packaged food.

5.5 Water for Health

Water is a vital part of our diet with several key functions:

  • Helps in digesting food.
  • Keeps skin and body healthy.
  • Removes waste from the body through urine, sweat, and faeces.
  • Can be sourced from drinking water and water-rich fruits like watermelon and cucumber.

5.6 Methods of Cooking

Different food items require different preparation methods to be palatable and healthy:

  • Steaming: e.g., Idli, Idiyappam
  • Boiling: e.g., Rice, Kheer, Sambhar
  • Frying: e.g., Samosa, Puri
  • Roasting: e.g., Papad
  • Baking: e.g., Cake, Muffins
  • Grilling: e.g., Roasted corn
  • Solar Cooker: An eco-friendly cooking method that uses sunlight instead of fire or electricity, producing no smoke or ash.
