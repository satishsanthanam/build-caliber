Class 5 — Chapter 9: Rhythms of Nature

9.1 Introduction: Understanding Change

Changes are constantly occurring in ourselves and the world around us. Some changes happen over years, while others occur in days, minutes, or even seconds.

  • Long-term changes: Growing taller, trees growing, and changes in our environment over years.
  • Short-term changes: Leaves falling and growing back, flowers blooming and drying, the sky changing color, and the daily cycle of the sun.
9.2 Changes Around Us in a Day (Day and Night)

The daily transition from day to night and back to day is one of the most prominent natural rhythms.

What Causes Day and Night?

Although the Sun appears to rise in the east, move across the sky, and set in the west, it is actually the Earth that is moving, not the Sun.

  • Rotation: The Earth slowly turns around (rotates) in place.
  • As the Earth rotates, the side facing the Sun experiences day, while the side facing away from the Sun experiences night.

The Globe as a Model of Earth

A globe is a spherical model that represents our Earth.

  • The blue parts of the globe represent seas and oceans, which cover about $\frac{3}{4}$ of the Earth's surface.
  • Seas are smaller water bodies partly enclosed by land, whereas oceans are vast, open water bodies.
  • The remaining parts of the globe represent landmasses where countries are located.
9.3 Changes Around Us in a Year (Seasons)

Nature changes slowly over the course of a year in a repeating pattern called seasons.

The Six Seasons of India

In India, nature's cycle is traditionally divided into six distinct seasons based on climatic changes every few months:

  1. Vasanta (Spring)
  2. Grishma (Summer)
  3. Varsha (Monsoon)
  4. Sharad (Autumn)
  5. Hemant (Pre-winter)
  6. Shishir (Winter)

Agricultural Cycles and Seasons

Farmers align their crop cultivation with seasonal patterns because different crops require different conditions to grow:

  • Summer Crops: Crops that grow best in the heat of summer.
  • Winter Crops: Crops that grow best in the cooler winter months.
  • Rainy Season Crops: Crops planted during the monsoon because they require abundant water to grow.

Festivals and Seasons

Many cultural festivals in India are closely linked to seasonal changes and harvest cycles:

  • Pongal & Makar Sankranti: Celebrated in Winter (Harvest festival).
  • Holi: Celebrated in Spring (When flowers bloom).
  • Diwali: Celebrated in Autumn (After crops are harvested).
  • Baisakhi, Gudi Padwa, Vishu, & Rongali Bihu: Springtime New Year and harvest festivals.
