Class 5 — Chapter 7: Energy—How Things Work

7.1 What is Energy?

Around us, things move, light up, make sounds, or get warmer or cooler. Something makes this all happen—we call that energy. Energy is what makes things move, light up, produce sound, do work and change temperature.

Exploratory Activities

  • Activity 1: Balloon Air Rocket — When air rushes out of a balloon, it pushes it forward, demonstrating how the movement of air generates energy.
  • Activity 2: Rubber Band Guitar — Plucking stretched rubber bands makes them vibrate and produce sounds, demonstrating sound energy.
  • Activity 3: Sun-powered Water Warmer — Water kept in sunlight becomes warmer, demonstrating how sunlight provides heat energy.
7.2 Sources of Energy

Energy comes from various sources depending on the system:

  • Food: The source of energy for all living things (humans and animals) to move, run, and perform daily activities.
  • Fuel: Materials like petrol, diesel, cooking gas, wood, and coal are fuels that act as sources of energy for vehicles and cooking.

Activity 4: Understanding How Fuel Works

Comparing a dry cotton wick with a wick soaked in oil (diya) shows that the oil acts as a fuel to keep the flame burning longer.

7.3 Electricity

Electricity is a highly versatile form of energy used to run appliances, machines, and industries. It can be converted into movement, sound, light, and heat.

Safe Use of Electricity

  • Do not touch wires or electrical devices when plugged in.
  • Do not put fingers or objects into electrical sockets.
  • Stay away from broken wires or fallen electric poles and inform an adult immediately.
  • Do not play near electric boxes or transformers.

Energy Efficiency

Energy efficiency means using less energy to do the same work (e.g., using LED bulbs instead of incandescent bulbs to save electricity).

7.4 Generating Clean Energy

Most electricity currently comes from burning fossil fuels like coal, which causes air pollution. However, we can generate electricity from natural, non-polluting sources.

  • Sun: Solar panels capture sunlight and turn it into electricity.
  • Wind: Windmills spin with the wind to generate electricity.
  • Water: Fast-moving rivers or falling water are used to generate electricity.

Electricity generated from these natural sources is called clean energy because it does not pollute the planet with smoke or waste.

Activities on Natural Energy

  • Activity 6: Pinwheel — Demonstrates how wind energy causes rotation.
  • Activity 7: Sunlight in Focus — Uses a magnifying glass to focus solar energy on paper.
  • Activity 8: Water Wheel — Demonstrates how flowing water can make a wheel spin.
7.5 Energy Flow and Storage

Our body stores energy for future use, allowing us to function even if we skip a meal. Similarly, batteries contain stored energy to power devices.

Traditional Knowledge: Vaastu Shastra

Vaastu Shastra is an ancient Indian system of design and architecture that helps plan buildings in harmony with nature, optimizing the use of natural light, wind, and heat to reduce energy consumption.

7.6 Let Us Reflect
  • Why is it better to use solar or wind energy instead of coal?
  • What are some examples of stored energy?
  • How can we practice energy conservation at home?
