Class 3 — Chapter 8: Food We Eat

8.1 My Favourite Food

We all like to eat and drink different things. Some of these food items are sweet, some are sour, some are spicy, and some are bitter. We eat fruits, vegetables, rice, dal, or roti. We drink milk, lassi, or juice.

Chhappan Bhog

Chhappan bhog is a special food prepared on festivals and auspicious occasions in honour of guests, friends, and family. It contains 56 varieties of food items representing a harmonious blend of six tastes:

  • Sweet (meetha)
  • Spicy (teekha)
  • Astringent (kasaila)
  • Sour (khatta)
  • Salty (namkeen)
  • Bitter (karva)
8.2 Story of Shirin — the Runner

Shirin was in Class 3 and used to run very fast. However, she often fell sick. When she asked a champion runner how to run like her and win medals, the champion asked about her diet. Shirin revealed she only liked to eat rice and potatoes.

The Champion's Advice

The champion advised Shirin that to have a strong and healthy body, she must eat a variety of foods:

  • Eat different kinds of vegetables and fruits.
  • Eat things made from different kinds of grains, like rice, ragi, jowar, wheat, and bajra.
  • Eat pulses (dal) and nuts.
  • Eat food cooked at home.

Shirin followed this advice, understood the value of a balanced diet, and soon felt energetic, healthy, and won a medal for her school.

8.3 We Eat Different Things

Elders in our family cook different foods during summer and winter. We get different fruits and vegetables in different seasons.

  • Summer: We love to eat kulfi or ice-cream, drink tender coconut water, and eat mangoes and melons.
  • Winter: We eat warm food, soups, and stew, enjoy hot drinks, and eat plenty of apples.

Summer Drinks

During the summer, the weather is hot and we sweat a lot, losing water from our body. To stay hydrated, we should drink water frequently and consume drinks like:

  • Tender Coconut Water
  • Mango Juice
  • Aam Panna
  • Orange Juice
  • Lime Water (nimbu pani)
  • Sugarcane juice
  • Buttermilk

Our body needs a lot of water. We cannot live without water, which is why we say, 'water is life'.

8.4 Where Does Food Come From?

We get our food from both plants and animals.

Parts of Plants We Eat

  • Leaves: Spinach (Palak), Fenugreek (Methi), Cabbage, Mustard, Coriander (Dhaniya).
  • Fruits: Guava, Sapota, Apple, Mango.
  • Roots: Carrot, Radish, Beetroot.
  • Stems: Potatoes, Bamboo shoots, Ginger, Onion.
  • Seeds: Grains, Pulses, Dry fruits.

Food from Animals

Some people eat things that are taken from animals, such as milk, curd, ghee, cheese, paneer, honey, eggs, and meat.

Puzzle Time

Two of our most commonly used ingredients are neither obtained from plants nor from animals. These are Salt and Water.

8.5 Let Us Reflect

Review and reflection exercises based on the chapter:

  • Discuss: Why do we need different kinds of food? Why do we eat seasonal foods? How can you tell if a food is healthy or unhealthy? How can we reduce food waste at home?
  • Write: List the fruits and vegetables eaten in your family. Write the recipe of your favourite dish. Name a food item that includes both plant and animal-based ingredients (e.g., lassi).
  • Draw: Draw three plates or thalis with food items you eat in the morning, afternoon, and at night.
  • Think: Imagine you suddenly have a guest visiting your home. What kind of food would you serve them and why?
