Chapter 11: Making Things

11.1 Introduction

We use many clay objects in our daily lives, such as a pot for storing water, a gullak (piggy bank), a birdbath, and a kulhad (clay cup). These objects are made of clay by skilled craftspeople.

11.2 A Potter's Family

11.2.1 The Process of Pot Making

Pottery involves several detailed steps to turn raw clay into durable vessels:

  • Preparing the Clay: The clay is mixed with water to achieve the right consistency, pounded well, and kneaded like dough.
  • Shaping: The kneaded clay is placed on a spinning stone wheel (potter's wheel) and shaped by hand.
  • Baking: Once dried, the pots are baked in a very hot oven called a kiln to make them strong and durable.
  • Decorating: Beautiful patterns and colors are painted on the pots to make them look attractive.

11.2.2 Activity: Making Your Own Clay

You can make clay at home with these steps:

  1. Collect soil from wet or muddy places.
  2. Sieve the soil to remove pebbles, stones, sand, and leaves.
  3. Mix the soil with water in a flat vessel and let it settle.
  4. Pour away the excess water from the top and knead the remaining clay into a dough.
11.3 Patterns in Nature and Pottery

11.3.1 Patterns in Nature

Artists often get inspiration from natural patterns, such as:

  • Spots on a leopard.
  • Stripes on a zebra or squirrel.
  • Symmetrical arrangements of leaves on trees like the neem.

11.3.2 Patterns in Indian Pottery

Different regions of India have unique traditional pottery styles decorated with beautiful geometric and natural patterns.

11.4 Brick by Brick

11.4.1 How Bricks are Made

Like pots, bricks are made of mud and clay. They are shaped using moulds (similar to idli or cake moulds) and baked in a kiln to become hard. Bricks are essential for constructing strong buildings like houses, schools, and hospitals.

11.4.2 Types of Houses

Houses are built using different materials depending on the climate and region:

  • Mud Houses: Built in warm areas using natural materials like grass, wood, and clay. Walls and floors are often coated with cow dung to keep the interiors cool and prevent insects from entering.
  • Other Houses: Tents (made of canvas), skyscrapers (made of concrete, steel, and glass), stone houses, and houseboats.
11.5 Safety First

Construction workers follow strict safety rules to protect themselves, including wearing a helmet with a strap, a safety vest, and sturdy footwear.
