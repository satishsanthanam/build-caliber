Class 3 — Chapter 6: Living in Harmony

6.1 Introduction: Co-existing with Nature

We share our surroundings and homes with various living beings, including animals, birds, insects, and plants. Some of these are invited, while others are uninvited guests.

6.1.1 Sharing Our Homes

We bring some animals and plants into our homes intentionally and care for them by providing food, water, and shelter. However, some animals and insects visit our homes uninvited.

  • Invited/Domesticated: Pets like dogs and cats, or farm animals like cows and goats.
  • Uninvited Guests: Insects, spiders, or birds that visit or live in corners of our houses.
6.2 The Story of the Mango Tree

Through the story of Shambhu and his father, we learn how plants and animals support each other's survival.

6.2.1 Interdependence in Action

While sitting under a huge mango tree, Shambhu observes a monkey eating a mango and throwing away the seed. His father explains that a new plant will emerge from this seed, demonstrating how animals help plants reproduce and spread.

6.2.2 Major Uses of Plants

Plants are essential to life on Earth. Some of the key Uses of Plants include:

  • Shelter for animals: Providing homes for birds, squirrels, insects, and monkeys.
  • Shade: Offering cool shade for humans and animals.
  • Wood: Used for making furniture (like chairs) and houses.
  • Fibre: Used for making clothes and threads.
  • Fresh air: Producing oxygen for us to breathe.
  • Fruit & Vegetables: Providing food for humans and animals.
  • Medicine: Serving as sources for natural remedies.
6.3 We Need Each Other

All living things—humans, animals, and plants—are interconnected and rely on one another to survive and maintain balance in nature.

6.3.1 Mutual Dependence

  • How Humans Help: We provide care, protection, food, and shelter to domestic animals and cultivated plants.
  • How Animals Help Plants: Animals help in seed dispersal (spreading seeds to new places) and make the soil fertile through their waste.
  • How Plants Help Animals: Plants provide food, oxygen, and shelter.

6.3.2 Fun Fact: Squirrels as Planters

Thousands of trees are accidentally planted by squirrels because they bury nuts and forget where they hid them!

6.4 Activities and Observations

Practical activities to help students observe and build empathy towards animals and plants in their environment.

6.4.1 Activity 1: Get to Know an Animal

Students choose a local animal or bird (like a cow, dog, cat, crow, or sparrow) and observe its movements, sounds, eating habits, and social interactions.

6.4.2 Activity 2: Busy and Clever Ants!

An experiment to observe ant behavior by placing three types of food in a line:

  • Sweet: Sugar or jaggery.
  • Fried: Papad or murukku.
  • Boiled/Cooked: Bread, chapati, rice, or ragi.

Students predict and observe which food items the ants prefer and how they interact with them.

6.4.3 Activity 3: Visiting Your Plant Friend

An exercise in developing empathy where children are encouraged to make friends with a tree or plant, sharing their feelings (happiness, sadness, or anger) with it.

6.5 Exercises and Classification

Review questions and classification exercises to consolidate learning.

6.5.1 Classification by Number of Legs

Students classify animals, birds, and insects based on their physical structure:

  • Two legs: Birds (e.g., crows, sparrows, ducks).
  • Four legs: Mammals and reptiles (e.g., dogs, cats, cows, goats).
  • Six legs: Most insects (e.g., ants, grasshoppers, butterflies).
  • Eight legs: Arachnids (e.g., spiders).
6.6 A True Story: The Grey Wagtails of Valparai

A heartwarming real-life example of human-animal harmony from Tamil Nadu.

6.6.1 Welcoming the Migratory Birds

In the small town of Valparai in Tamil Nadu, children and teachers participate in welcoming migratory birds called Grey Wagtails (locally known as Vaalatti kuruvi in Tamil).

These birds travel all the way from the cold mountains to the warmer parts of India during the winter months. To celebrate their arrival, the local school children put up welcome posters and distribute sweets to everyone in the town.
