Class 3 — Chapter 1: Family and Friends

1.1 Playing Together

Families and communities provide a supportive environment where members share joy and activities.

  • Bela's Family: Consists of Bela, her brothers Banku Bhaiya and little Bishu.
  • Outdoor Games: They play chhupan-chhupai (hide and seek) and peek-a-boo in the open space outside their house.
  • Social Spaces: Their garden serves as a meeting place for relatives, friends, and neighbours to relax and socialize.
1.2 Singing Together and Rainy Days

Rainy days bring families together for indoor and sheltered activities.

  • Activities: Sitting under a garden shelter, playing games like antakshari and snakes and ladders.
  • Family Roles: Mother and Dadiji (grandmother) prepare hot pakodas and tea.
  • Cultural Expression: Dadiji sings traditional rain songs, teaching them to the children.

A Rain Song

A poem inspired by a folk song describing dark clouds, rain, paper boats in puddles, huddled birds, and dancing peacocks.

1.3 Laughing Together and Pets
  • Intergenerational Bonding: Dadaji (grandfather) teaches and plays traditional games from his childhood with the children.
  • Pets as Family: The pet dog, Shiru, is treated as an integral family member who plays, runs around, and shares food with the children.
1.4 Types of Families

Families come in various structures and sizes:

  • Diversity: Families can be big or small, with different members including parents, children, grandparents, and others.
  • Core Value: Regardless of size, family members care for and love one another.
1.5 Helping Each Other

Cooperation and mutual support are essential in a household and community.

  • Daily Chores: Family members share responsibilities such as cooking, cleaning, shopping for groceries, oiling hair, and gardening.
  • Caring for Nature: Families extend care to animals (cows, buffaloes, dogs, cats, goats) and plants.
  • Community Friends: Neighbors like Munni and Kusum Mausi support each other. Munni creates beautiful rangoli using natural materials like dried flowers and leaves.
1.6 Fun with Family and Friends

Recreational activities strengthen relationships.

  • Outings: Visiting the park with grandparents (Dadaji).
  • Traditional Games: Playing pitthoo (seven stones) in teams with neighborhood friends.
