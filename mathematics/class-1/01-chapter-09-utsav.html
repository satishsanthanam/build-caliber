Class 1 — Chapter 9: Utsav

9.1 Introduction to Utsav and Festivals

The festival of Uttarayan (which includes local celebrations like Makar Sankranti, Pongal, Bihu, and Lohri) marks the transition from winter to summer in the Indian calendar. This festive period signifies the return of sunny days and the beginning of the harvest season.

These celebrations are rich with cultural elements, including traditional decorations, music, dance, and food, which highlight various geometric shapes and repeating patterns.

9.2 Visual and Creative Patterns

9.2.1 Extending Visual Patterns

Patterns can be created and extended by repeating sequences of everyday objects and drawings:

  • Stones and Sticks: Alternating linear arrangements (e.g., stone, stick, stone, stick).
  • Festive Garlands (Toran): Alternating patterns of leaves, flowers, and hanging decorations.
  • Diyas and Candles: Alternating light sources (e.g., diya, candle, diya, candle).
  • Geometric Shapes: Repeating sequences of circles, triangles, squares, arrows, stars, and hexagons.

9.2.2 Fingerprint and Vegetable Printing

Creative patterns can be made using body impressions and natural objects:

  • Fingerprint Art: Using thumb and finger impressions to create animal shapes (cats, cows, chicks, frogs) and repeating color sequences (e.g., alternating pink and grey prints).
  • Vegetable Printing: Using sliced vegetables (such as ladyfinger, carrot, or capsicum) dipped in color to stamp repeating designs.
9.3 Number Patterns

Patterns can also be found in numbers by identifying the rule of sequence:

  • Odd Numbers: $1, 3, 5, 7, \underline{9}, 11, \underline{13}$ (adding $2$ each time).
  • Backward Counting: $22, 21, 20, \underline{19}, \underline{18}, 17, 16$ (subtracting $1$ each time).
  • Skip Counting by 10s: $10, 20, 30, \underline{40}, 50, \underline{60}$ (adding $10$ each time).
  • Repeating Digits / Skip Counting by 11s: $11, 22, 33, \underline{44}, \underline{55}, 66, \underline{77}$ (adding $11$ each time).
  • Skip Counting by 5s: $5, 10, \underline{15}, 20, \underline{25}$ (adding $5$ each time).
  • Growing Sequences: $2, 5, 8, 11, \underline{14}, \underline{17}, \underline{20}$ (adding $3$ each time).
9.4 Kolam, Rangoli, and Project Work

9.4.1 Kolam and Rangoli

Kolam or Rangoli is a traditional decorative art made using patterns of dots and lines, showcasing symmetry and geometric design.

9.4.2 Project Work & Activities

  • Pattern Collection: Collect pebbles, flowers, leaves, sticks, bangles, coins, and caps to arrange them in creative patterns.
  • Patterns in Nature: Observe patterns on leaves, butterflies, animal skins (zebra, tiger, cat), and beehives.
  • Action Patterns: Create rhythmic patterns using physical actions like clapping, snapping fingers, and stamping feet.
  • Heritage Exploration: Explore and appreciate beautiful patterns in historical monuments, temples, mosques, churches, and gurudwaras.
