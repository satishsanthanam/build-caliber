Class 3 — Chapter 5: Fun with Shapes

5.1 Introduction to Shapes and Rangoli

Shapes can be combined to create beautiful designs and patterns, such as a rangoli. These designs are often made on a grid of dots using a combination of straight and curved lines.

Lines in Shapes

  • Straight Lines: Used to form shapes like squares, rectangles, and triangles.
  • Curved Lines: Used to form shapes like circles and curved patterns in rangolis.
5.2 3D Objects and 2D Faces

Three-dimensional (3D) objects like boxes, matchboxes, and sharpeners are made up of flat surfaces called faces. When these objects are opened up or traced, they reveal two-dimensional (2D) shapes.

Flattening Boxes

Opening up a cardboard box carefully reveals its net, showing the 2D shapes that make up its faces. For example, a cuboidal box unfolds into several rectangular faces.

Tracing Faces

Tracing the outline of a 3D solid object on paper yields 2D shapes:

  • Tracing a matchbox or a cuboid yields rectangles.
  • Tracing some wooden boxes can yield both squares and rectangles.
5.3 Properties of Rectangles

A rectangle is a flat, four-sided shape with specific geometric properties.

Key Properties of a Rectangle

  • It has $4$ sides.
  • It has $4$ corners (vertices).
  • The opposite sides of a rectangle are equal in length.
5.4 Properties of Squares

A square is a special type of rectangle where all sides are equal.

Key Properties of a Square

  • It has $4$ sides.
  • It has $4$ corners.
  • All $4$ sides are equal in length.

Difference Between a Square and a Rectangle

While both shapes have $4$ sides and $4$ corners, in a square, all four sides must be equal in length. In a rectangle, only the opposite sides need to be equal in length.

5.5 Understanding Corners and Angles

The corners of shapes can be classified by comparing them to the corner of a square or rectangle, which is known as a square corner (right angle).

Types of Corners

  • Square Corner: The standard corner found in squares, rectangles, books, and tables.
  • Less than a Square Corner: An angle that is sharper/narrower than a square corner.
  • More than a Square Corner: An angle that is wider/more open than a square corner.
5.6 Properties of Triangles

A triangle is a three-sided closed shape.

Key Properties of a Triangle

  • It has $3$ sides.
  • It has $3$ corners.
5.7 Properties of Circles

A circle is a round shape with no straight edges or corners.

Finding the Center of a Circle

The center of a circular paper plate or sheet can be found using paper folding:

  1. Fold the circular sheet exactly in half to get a straight crease line (diameter).
  2. Fold it in half again along a different direction.
  3. Open the sheet. The point where the two crease lines meet is the center of the circle.
5.8 Tangrams and Patterns

Shapes can be fitted together to create complex figures, patterns, and borders.

Tangram

A tangram is a puzzle consisting of flat shapes (pieces) which are put together to form various target designs.

Borders and Line Patterns

Borders on sarees, walls, and doors are created by repeating patterns of straight lines, curves, and geometric shapes.
