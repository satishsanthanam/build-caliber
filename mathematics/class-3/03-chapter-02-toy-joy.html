Class 3 — Chapter 2: Toy Joy

2.1 Introduction to 3D Shapes

In this chapter, we explore various three-dimensional (3D) shapes found in everyday objects, toys, and crafts.

Key 3D Shapes

  • Cube: A box-like shape with six equal square faces (e.g., a die, building blocks).
  • Cuboid: A box-like shape with rectangular faces (e.g., a matchbox, textbook). Note that a cube is a special type of cuboid.
  • Cylinder: A round, tube-like shape with flat circular ends (e.g., bottles, cans, markers).
  • Cone: A shape with a circular base that tapers to a point (e.g., ice cream cone, funnel, party hat).
  • Sphere: A perfectly round ball-like shape (e.g., football, globe, orange).

Parts of a 3D Shape

  • Face: The flat or curved surface of the shape.
  • Edge: The line segment where two faces meet.
  • Corner (Vertex): The point where three or more edges meet.
2.2 Properties and Classification of Shapes

Shapes can be classified by analyzing their faces, edges, and corners:

  • With no edges: Sphere
  • With only flat faces: Cube and Cuboid
  • With only curved faces: Sphere
  • With both straight and curved edges: None (Standard cylinders and cones have only curved edges, while cubes and cuboids have only straight edges).
  • With both flat and curved faces: Cylinder (two flat faces, one curved face) and Cone (one flat face, one curved face).
2.3 Building and Combining Shapes

Building with Clay and Sticks

We can build 3D shape frames using clay balls (to represent corners/vertices) and sticks (to represent edges). For example, a cube frame requires $8$ clay balls and $12$ sticks.

Joining Cubes

When we join multiple cubes together, we can form new shapes:

  • Joining a cube and a cuboid results in a larger Cuboid.
  • Three cubes can be joined in different configurations to form various L-shaped or straight-line 3D structures.
2.4 Fun with Dice

A standard die is a cube with faces numbered from $1$ to $6$ dots. The opposite faces of a standard die always follow a specific mathematical pattern:

  • The sum of the dots on opposite faces is always equal to $7$.
  • Opposite to $1$ is $6$ ($1 + 6 = 7$)
  • Opposite to $2$ is $5$ ($2 + 5 = 7$)
  • Opposite to $3$ is $4$ ($3 + 4 = 7$)
