Class 3 — Chapter 13: Time Goes On

13.1 The Calendar Game

Calendars help us keep track of days, weeks, and months throughout the year. By studying calendars, we can observe patterns in how time is structured.

Understanding Months and Days

  • A standard year has 12 months.
  • Months have different numbers of days: some have $30$ days, some have $31$ days, and February has $28$ or $29$ days.
  • A week consists of 7 days: Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, and Sunday.

Calendar Exploration Activities

By comparing calendars from different years, we can observe what remains the same and what changes:

  • Same: Names of the months, days in a week, and the sequence of months.
  • Changes: The specific day of the week on which a particular date falls, and the total number of Sundays in a specific month.
  • Yearly Duration: A standard year has $365$ days, which is approximately 52 weeks.
13.2 Age Fun and Timelines

We can use mathematics to calculate ages and understand family generations by comparing birth years and current years.

Age Word Problems

Consider the following puzzle: Hetal is twice as old as her brother. She is also $10$ years older than her brother. What are their ages?

  • Let the brother's age be $x$.
  • Hetal's age is $2x$ and also $x + 10$.
  • Therefore, $2x = x + 10 \implies x = 10$.
  • Hetal is $20$ years old, and her brother is $10$ years old.

Birth Certificates

A Birth Certificate is an official document recording the details of a person's birth, including:

  • Name and Sex
  • Date of Birth (written in figures and words, e.g., $02/05/2015$ as Second May Two Thousand Fifteen)
  • Place of Birth
  • Parents' Names
  • Date of Registration and Registration Number
13.3 Telling Time with Clocks

Clocks are instruments used to measure and indicate time in hours and minutes.

Analog Clocks

An Analog Clock uses moving hands to show time on a circular dial:

  • Hour Hand: The shorter hand that indicates the hour.
  • Minute Hand: The longer hand that indicates the minutes past the hour.
  • Reading Time: When the hour hand is at $7$ and the minute hand is at $12$, the time is $7:00$ (7 o'clock). If the minute hand moves to $3$, it represents $15$ minutes past the hour ($7:15$ or quarter past 7).

Daily Routines and Time Intervals

Tracking daily activities helps us understand the duration of tasks:

  • Eating breakfast: $7:00$ to $7:15$ takes $15$ minutes.
  • School periods, lunch breaks, and playtimes are typically measured in minutes.
13.4 Measuring Durations of Time

Different events in our lives take different amounts of time to complete, ranging from minutes to months.

Time Categories

  • Takes Minutes: Brushing teeth, taking a shower, blinking eyes.
  • Takes Hours: Stitching a shirt, school day, watching a movie.
  • Takes Days: Knitting a sweater, a short trip, painting a house.
  • Takes Weeks: Growing small plants (like radish), school holidays.
  • Takes Months: Changing seasons (summer to winter), growing crops (like wheat or cabbage).

Modern and Ancient Timekeepers

  • Digital Clock: Shows time directly using numerical digits (e.g., $18:02$).
  • Sand Clock (Timer): An ancient device consisting of two glass bulbs connected by a narrow neck, allowing sand to flow from the top to the bottom over a fixed duration.
