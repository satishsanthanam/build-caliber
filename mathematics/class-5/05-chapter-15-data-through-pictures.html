15.2 Stock-Taking in a Shop

Joseph Uncle takes stock of the play items (toys, board games, and sports items) in his store. He uses a pictograph to record the items. Since there are too many items, his helper Dipesh suggested using a scale where one picture (icon) represents multiple items.

Pictograph Scale

  • 🚜 (Toy Icon) = $5$ toys
  • 🎲 (Board Game Icon) = $5$ board games
  • 🏸 (Sports Item Icon) = $5$ sports items

15.4 Recording a Day (Pictograph vs. Bar Graph)

Raman and Sheela recorded how they spent their 24-hour day using different methods.

Raman's Pictorial Record

Raman used a table where 1 blue square represents $1$ hour:

  • Time spent sleeping: 8 hours (8 squares)
  • Time in school: 6 hours (6 squares)
  • Time spent studying: 2 hours (2 squares)
  • Time spent eating and playing: 6 hours (6 squares)
  • Other activities: 1 hour (1 square)

Sheela's Bar Graph

Sheela represented her routine using a bar graph, where the height of vertical bars represents the number of hours spent on each activity:

  • Sleeping: 8 hours
  • Time in school: 7 hours
  • Studying: 4 hours
  • Eating and playing: 3 hours
  • Other activities: 2 hours

A bar graph uses rectangular bars of uniform width with lengths proportional to the values they represent.

Comparison Questions

  1. Whose daily routine shows more time spent on sleeping?
  2. Who spends more hours in the school?
  3. How many more hours does Sheela spend studying compared to Raman?
  4. Is there any activity on which they spend the same amount of time? If yes, name the activity.
  5. Based on their data, whose routine do you think is more balanced? Why?
15.5 Food Wastage in the School Canteen

Rani's school dining hall displays a bar graph showing the amount of food wasted (in kg) over weekdays for different food items.

Wastage Data

  • Sandwich (Monday): $5\text{ kg}$
  • Paratha (Tuesday): $6\text{ kg}$
  • Khichdi (Wednesday): $8\text{ kg}$
  • Puri-Sabzi (Thursday): $7\text{ kg}$
  • Idli-Sambar (Friday): $4\text{ kg}$

Analysis Questions

  1. Which food item had the highest amount of wastage?
  2. Which food item had the least amount of wastage?
  3. How much total food wastage was recorded in these days?
  4. If $1\text{ kg}$ of food waste can feed $3$ children, how many children could have been fed with the total food wasted?
  5. _______ day had less food wastage than _______ day.
  6. If the same food items are to be repeated next week, can you predict which food item is likely to be wasted the most?
15.6 True or False Observation Activity

Based on observing a group of children, determine whether the following statements are True or False:

  • All girls are wearing glasses.
  • 10 children are wearing caps on their heads.
  • More boys are holding books than girls.
  • More than half of the children are wearing glasses.
  • The number of children holding books is greater than the number of children wearing caps.
  • The number of boys wearing glasses is greater than the number of girls wearing glasses.
  • Some children are wearing both glasses and caps.
  • There is no child who has all three items—a cap, a book, and a backpack.
