Class 8 (Part 1) — Chapter 1: A Square and A Cube

1.1 Square Numbers

Properties of Square Numbers

  • A square number (or perfect square) is a number that can be expressed as the product of a number with itself (n × n = n²).
  • Ending Digits: Perfect squares only end with 0, 1, 4, 5, 6, or 9 in their units place. They never end with 2, 3, 7, or 8.
  • Unit Digit Rules:
    • If a number ends in 1 or 9, its square ends in 1.
    • If a number ends in 4 or 6, its square ends in 6.
    • If a number ends in 5, its square ends in 5.
  • Number of Zeros: If a number ends with k zeros, its square ends with 2k zeros (always an even number of zeros).
  • Parity: The square of an even number is always even, and the square of an odd number is always odd.

Interesting Patterns

  • Sum of Consecutive Odd Numbers: The sum of the first n consecutive odd natural numbers starting from 1 is equal to n².
    Example: 1 + 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 = 25 = 5².
  • Numbers Between Consecutive Squares: There are 2n non-perfect square numbers lying between the squares of consecutive numbers n² and (n+1)².
  • Triangular Numbers: Numbers whose dot patterns can be arranged as triangles (1, 3, 6, 10, 15...). The sum of two consecutive triangular numbers is a square number.
    Example: 3 + 6 = 9 = 3².
1.2 Square Roots

Finding Square Roots

  • The square root is the inverse operation of squaring. If y = x², then x = √y.
  • Methods to Find Square Roots:
    1. Successive Subtraction: Subtract consecutive odd numbers starting from 1. If you reach 0 at the n-th step, the square root is n.
    2. Prime Factorisation: Resolve the number into prime factors. Group them into identical pairs and take one factor from each pair.
    3. Estimation: Estimate by finding the nearest known perfect squares and narrowing down the units digit.
1.3 Cubic Numbers & Cube Roots

Perfect Cubes

  • A perfect cube is obtained by multiplying a number by itself three times (n × n × n = n³).
  • Properties of Cubes:
    • Cubes can end in any digit (0-9).
    • If a number ends in k zeros, its cube ends in 3k zeros (always a multiple of 3). A perfect cube cannot end in exactly two zeros.
  • Taxicab Numbers: Numbers that can be expressed as the sum of two cubes in two different ways. The smallest is 1729 (Hardy-Ramanujan Number):
    1729 = 1³ + 12³ = 9³ + 10³.
  • Consecutive Odd Numbers Pattern: Cubes can be expressed as sums of consecutive odd numbers.
    Example: 1³ = 1, 2³ = 3 + 5, 3³ = 7 + 9 + 11.

Cube Roots

  • The cube root is the inverse of cubing, denoted by ³√y.
  • Prime Factorisation Method: Group prime factors into triplets. Take one factor from each triplet to find the cube root.
1.4 Historical Notes & Summary
  • Babylonians (1700 BCE): Compiled the first known lists of perfect squares and cubes on clay tablets.
  • Sanskrit Terminology:
    • Varga = Square / Square power
    • Ghana = Solid cube / Cube power
    • Mula = Root (origin of "square root")
