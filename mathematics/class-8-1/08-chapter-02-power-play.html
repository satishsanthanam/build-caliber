Class 8 (Part 1) — Chapter 2: Power Play

2.1 Experiencing the Power Play

Exponential vs. Linear Growth
  • Exponential Growth: Also known as multiplicative growth, where a quantity increases by a constant factor over equal intervals (e.g., doubling the thickness of a paper with each fold).
  • Linear Growth: Additive growth where a quantity increases by a constant fixed amount at each step.
  • The Folding Phenomenon: A sheet of paper of thickness 0.001 cm folded 46 times would theoretically reach a thickness exceeding the distance to the Moon (over 7,00,000 km).
2.2 Exponential Notation and Operations

Core Definitions
  • Exponential Notation: Written as na, representing the base n multiplied by itself a times.
  • Base: The number that is being repeatedly multiplied (e.g., in 54, 5 is the base).
  • Exponent / Power: The number of times the base is multiplied (e.g., in 54, 4 is the exponent).
  • Negative Bases:
    • (-1)odd number = -1 (Negative)
    • (-1)even number = 1 (Positive)

Laws of Exponents
  • Product Law: na × nb = na+b
  • Power of a Power: (na)b = nab
  • Power of a Product: ma × na = (mn)a
  • Power of a Quotient: ma ÷ na = (m/n)a
2.3 The Other Side of Powers

Division, Zero, and Negative Exponents
  • Quotient Law: na ÷ nb = na-b (where n ≠ 0)
  • Zero Exponent: Any non-zero base raised to the power of 0 is equal to 1: n0 = 1 (where n ≠ 0).
  • Negative Exponents: Represent the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive power: n-a = 1 / na and na = 1 / n-a (where n ≠ 0).
2.4 Powers of 10 & Scientific Notation

Expanded Form and Scientific Notation
  • Expanded Form: Writing numbers using powers of 10 to represent place values (e.g., 172 = 1 × 102 + 7 × 101 + 2 × 100).
  • Scientific Notation (Standard Form): Expressing very large or very small numbers in the form x × 10y, where 1 ≤ x < 10 and y is an integer.
  • Examples:
    • 5900 = 5.9 × 103
    • 14,33,50,00,00,000 m = 1.4335 × 1012 m
2.5 Historical Context & Large Numbers

Ancient Indian Number Systems
  • Lalitavistara: A Buddhist treatise detailing names for odd powers of ten up to 1053 (e.g., ayuta = 109, niyuta = 1011, tallakshana = 1053).
  • Traditional Indian Scale:
    • Lakh = 105
    • Crore = 107
    • Arab = 109
    • Kharab = 1011
    • Neel = 1013
    • Padma = 1015
    • Shankh = 1017
  • Googol: The number 10100.
