Chapter 5: Number Play

5.1 Is This a Multiple Of?

Sum of Consecutive Numbers

  • Parity rules: odd ± odd = even, even ± even = even, odd ± even = odd.
  • Expressions like a ± b ± c ± d maintain the same parity regardless of sign placement.

Pairs to Make Fours

  • Adding two even numbers that are multiples of 4 gives a multiple of 4.
  • Adding two even numbers that are NOT multiples of 4 (remainder 2) also gives a multiple of 4.

Divisibility Properties

  • If a divides M and N, then a divides M + N and M - N.
  • If A is divisible by k, all multiples of A are divisible by k.
  • If A is divisible by k, A is divisible by all factors of k.
  • If A is divisible by k and m, A is divisible by the LCM of k and m.

5.2 Checking Divisibility Quickly

  • Divisibility by 10, 5, 2: Depends entirely on the units digit.
  • Divisibility by 9 and 3: A number is divisible if the sum of its digits is divisible by 9 or 3.
  • Divisibility by 11: A number is divisible if the difference between the sum of digits at odd places and even places is 0 or a multiple of 11.
  • Divisibility by Composite Numbers (e.g., 6, 24): Check divisibility by their co-prime factors (e.g., 2 and 3 for 6; 3 and 8 for 24).

Digital Roots

  • A Digital Root is a single-digit number obtained by repeatedly adding the digits of a number.
  • The digital root of a number equals its remainder when divided by 9 (if the root is 9, the remainder is 0).

5.3 Digits in Disguise

  • Cryptarithms are puzzles where each letter stands for a unique digit, and the first digit of a number is never 0.

Figure it Out - Solutions

Q: Sum of 4 consecutive numbers is 34. What are they?

7, 8, 9, 10.

Q: Greatest of 5 consecutive numbers is p. Describe the others.

p-1, p-2, p-3, p-4.

Q: True/False Statements
  • Sum of two even numbers is a multiple of 3: Sometimes true (e.g., 2+4=6, but 2+6=8).
  • If not divisible by 18, not divisible by 9: Sometimes true (27 is not divisible by 18, but is by 9).
  • If two numbers are not divisible by 6, their sum is not divisible by 6: Sometimes true (9+11=20 not div by 6, but 8+10=18 is).
  • Sum of multiple of 6 and multiple of 9 is multiple of 3: Always true (6x + 9y = 3(2x+3y)).
  • Sum of multiple of 6 and multiple of 3 is multiple of 9: Sometimes true (18+9=27 is, 12+9=21 is not).
Q: Number leaving remainder 2 when divided by 3 and 4.

General form: 12n + 2 (e.g., 14, 26, 38).

Q: Pebble grouping puzzle (remainders 1 for 3 and 5, 0 for 7, less than 100).

91 pebbles.

Q: Sum of three numbers leaving remainder 2 when divided by 6 is a multiple of 6.

True. (6a+2) + (6b+2) + (6c+2) = 6(a+b+c+1).

Q: Remainders of 4779 + 661 and 4779 - 661 divided by 7.

Remainders are 1 and 2 respectively.

Q: Smallest number leaving remainder 2(div 3), 3(div 4), 4(div 5).

59 (LCM of 3,4,5 is 60; 60 - 1 = 59).

Q: Smallest multiple of 9 with no odd digits.

288.

Q: Multiple of 9 closest to 6000.

6003.

Q: Digital root of 9a + 36b + 13.

4.

Q: 31z5 is a multiple of 9. Value of z?

z = 0 or 9.

Q: 48a23b is a multiple of 18. Possible pairs for a and b.

(1,0), (8,2), (6,4), (4,6), (2,8).

Q: 3p7q8 is divisible by 44. Pairs for p and q.

(7,0), (5,2), (3,4), (1,6).

Q: Product of 2, 3, 4, 5 consecutive integers.

2 integers: multiple of 2. 3 integers: multiple of 6. 4 integers: multiple of 24. 5 integers: multiple of 120.
