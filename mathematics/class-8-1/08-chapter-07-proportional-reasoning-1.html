Class 8 (Part 1) — Chapter 7: Proportional Reasoning-1

7.1 Observing Similarity in Change & Ratios

Concept of Proportionality

  • When scaling digital images, if both width and height change by the same multiplicative factor, the images look similar and are said to be proportional.
  • If they change by different factors (or by equal additive/subtractive amounts), the image becomes distorted.

Ratios

  • A ratio represents a proportional relationship between two quantities, written as $a : b$.
  • The numbers $a$ and $b$ are called the terms of the ratio.
  • For every $a$ units of the first quantity, there are $b$ units of the second quantity.
7.2 Ratios in Simplest Form & Proportion

Simplest Form of a Ratio

  • To reduce a ratio to its simplest form, divide both terms by their Highest Common Factor (HCF).
  • Example: The ratio $60 : 40$ has an HCF of $20$. Dividing both terms by $20$ yields the simplest form $3 : 2$.

Proportion

  • When two ratios are equal in their simplest forms, they are in proportion.
  • This is denoted by the symbol :: (e.g., $a : b :: c : d$, meaning $a : b$ is proportional to $c : d$).
  • Cross-Multiplication Rule: If $a : b :: c : d$, then $ad = bc$.
  • Any of the four terms can be found if the other three are known: $d = \frac{bc}{a}$.
7.3 Trairasika — The Rule of Three

Historical Context (Aryabhata)

  • In ancient Indian mathematics, problems of proportionality were solved using the Rule of Three (Trairasika).
  • It involves three known terms to find a fourth unknown term:
    • pramāṇa: The standard measure ($a$)
    • phala: The fruit/result of the measure ($b$)
    • ichchhā: The requisition/desired value ($c$)
    • ichchhāphala: The desired yield ($d$)
  • Formula: $\text{ichchhāphala} = \frac{\text{phala} \times \text{ichchhā}}{\text{pramāṇa}}$
7.4 Sharing in a Ratio

Dividing a Quantity Proportionally

  • To divide a total quantity $x$ into two parts in the ratio $m : n$:
    • Total number of parts = $m + n$
    • Size of each part = $\frac{x}{m + n}$
    • First share = $m \times \frac{x}{m + n}$
    • Second share = $n \times \frac{x}{m + n}$
7.5 Unit Conversions

Standard Conversion Factors

  • Length: $1\text{ metre} = 3.281\text{ feet}$
  • Area:
    • $1\text{ square metre} = 10.764\text{ square feet}$
    • $1\text{ acre} = 43,560\text{ square feet}$
    • $1\text{ hectare} = 10,000\text{ square metres} = 2.471\text{ acres}$
  • Volume: $1\text{ litre} = 1,000\text{ mL} = 1,000\text{ cc}$
  • Temperature:
    • $\text{Fahrenheit} = \frac{9}{5} \times \text{Celsius} + 32$
    • $\text{Celsius} = \frac{5}{9} \times (\text{Fahrenheit} - 32)$
