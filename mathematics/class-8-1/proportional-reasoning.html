Chapter 7: Proportional Reasoning-1

7.1 & 7.2 Ratios and Similarity

  • Ratio: Represents proportional relationships (e.g., width to height). Formatted as a : b.
  • Terms: The numbers in a ratio (e.g., 60 and 40).
  • Proportional: When quantities change by the same factor (multiplication/division).

7.3 Ratios in their Simplest Form

  • Reduce ratios by dividing both terms by their HCF (Highest Common Factor).
  • Example: 60:40 divided by 20 (HCF) = 3:2.

7.4 Problem Solving with Proportional Reasoning

  • Proportion: When two ratios are equal in their simplest forms. Indicated by the :: symbol.
  • Example: a : b :: c : d means the ratios are proportional.
  • Cross Multiplication: If a : b :: c : d, then ad = bc. Therefore, d = bc / a.
  • Trairasika (Rule of Three): Ancient Indian method by Aryabhata to find a fourth unknown quantity using three knowns: pramana : phala :: ichchha : ichchhaphala.

7.5 Sharing, but Not Equally

  • To divide a quantity x in the ratio m : n:
  • Total parts = m + n.
  • First part = m × x / (m + n).
  • Second part = n × x / (m + n).

7.6 Unit Conversions

  • Length: 1 metre = 3.281 feet
  • Area: 1 sq metre = 10.764 sq feet | 1 acre = 43,560 sq feet | 1 hectare = 10,000 sq metres = 2.471 acres
  • Volume: 1 mL = 1 cc | 1 litre = 1,000 mL
  • Temperature: F = (9/5 × C) + 32 | C = 5/9 × (F - 32)

Summary

  • Ratios a : b indicate for every a units of the first quantity, there are b units of the second.
  • Two ratios a : b and c : d are proportional if their terms change by the same factor (i.e., ad = bc).
