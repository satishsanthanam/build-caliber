Class 4 — Chapter 4: Thousands Around Us

4.1 Introduction: Thousands Around Us

Jaspreet and Gulnaz are organising a langar (a community lunch) in their neighbourhood Gurudwara. They expect around one thousand ($1000$) people to be fed. This chapter introduces numbers up to four digits, exploring their representation, place value, and comparison.

Donations and Representations

Donations received for the langar are represented in various mathematical forms:

  • Wheat Flour: $4 \text{ Ones} + 7 \text{ Tens} = 74$
  • Pumpkins: $100 - 7 = 93$
  • HTO Blocks: Used to represent 3-digit numbers like $145$ ($1 \text{ Hundred} + 4 \text{ Tens} + 5 \text{ Ones}$) and $325$.
4.2 Visualizing One Thousand (1000)

Using Dienes Blocks, we can systematically build up to one thousand by grouping in tens:

  • 10 Ones = 1 Ten ($10 \times 1 = 10$)
  • 10 Tens = 1 Hundred ($10 \times 10 = 100$)
  • 10 Hundreds = 1 Thousand ($10 \times 100 = 1000$)

Zoltán Pál Dienes, a Hungarian mathematician, developed these blocks to help students understand the base-10 place value system.

Breaking Up One Thousand

Decomposing $1000$ into addition facts:

  • $900 + 100 = 1000$
  • $800 + 200 = 1000$
  • $850 + 150 = 1000$
  • $760 + 240 = 1000$
  • $1000 - 600 = 400$
4.3 Grouping and Regrouping

Regrouping allows us to simplify representations of numbers when a place value column exceeds 9:

  • Example 1: $11 \text{ Tens and } 14 \text{ Ones} = 1 \text{ Hundred} + 2 \text{ Tens} + 4 \text{ Ones} = 124$
  • Example 2: $1 \text{ Hundred, } 12 \text{ Tens and } 14 \text{ Ones} = 2 \text{ Hundreds} + 3 \text{ Tens} + 4 \text{ Ones} = 234$

Expanded Form

Writing a number as the sum of the values of its digits:

  • $3452 = 3000 + 400 + 50 + 2$ (Three thousand four hundred fifty-two)
  • $4085 = 4000 + 80 + 5$ (Four thousand eighty-five)
4.5 Comparing and Ordering Numbers

To compare two 4-digit numbers, we compare their digits from left to right (starting from the Thousands place):

  • Rule: Compare the Thousands place first. If they are equal, compare the Hundreds place, then Tens, and finally Ones.
  • Example: $3102 > 3012$ because the digit in the Hundreds place of $3102$ ($1$) is greater than that of $3012$ ($0$).

Ordering Real-World Data

We can arrange quantities like mountain heights or cricket scores in ascending or descending order:

  • K2: $8611 \text{ m}$
  • Kangchenjunga: $8586 \text{ m}$
  • Nanda Devi: $7816 \text{ m}$
  • Chaukhamba I: $7138 \text{ m}$
4.6 Real-World Applications of Thousands
  • Saavira Kambada Basadi: A famous Jain temple in Moodubidiri, Karnataka, built in the 1500s, featuring $1000$ unique pillars.
  • Indian Rhinoceros: Population recovered from around $200$ to nearly $4000$ due to conservation efforts.
  • India's Geography: India has $788$ districts, over $7500 \text{ km}$ of coastline, and a history spanning over $5000$ years.
