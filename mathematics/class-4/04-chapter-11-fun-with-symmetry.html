Class 4 — Chapter 11: Fun with Symmetry

11.1 Introduction to Symmetry

Symmetry is observed in various everyday objects such as rangolis, masks, bead strings, and buildings. A shape or design is said to be symmetrical if it can be divided into two equal halves that match each other perfectly.

The dividing line is called the line of symmetry or mirror line (also known as the line of reflection).

11.2 Hands-on Activities: Ink Designs & Paper Airplanes

11.2.1 Ink Design

An easy way to create a symmetrical pattern is through an ink design:

  • Step 1: Take a sheet of paper and fold it in half.
  • Step 2: Open it and sprinkle some drops of watercolours in the centre of the fold.
  • Step 3: Press it to spread the colour evenly.
  • Step 4: Open the paper to reveal a symmetrical pattern divided by the fold line (the line of symmetry).

11.2.2 Making a Paper Airplane

Folding a paper airplane involves symmetrical steps. By analyzing the folding steps, we can identify lines of symmetry in different stages of the paper plane. A plane may not fly properly if it lacks a clear line of symmetry, as asymmetry affects its balance.

11.3 Holes and Cuts

Creating patterns by folding paper and making cuts is a great way to explore symmetry:

  • Challenge 1: Predicting where holes and cuts will appear when a folded paper with corner/side cuts is opened.
  • Challenge 2: Determining the number of sides a shape will have after putting cuts in a folded paper and opening it.
  • Challenge 3: Figuring out where to cut a twice-folded paper to make a square hole in the center, and counting the number of cuts required.
11.4 Symmetry in Shapes and Mirror Games

11.4.1 Completing Designs on Dot Grids

Symmetrical designs can be completed on a dot grid by mirroring the given half across the line of symmetry.

11.4.2 Games with a Mirror

Using a mirror helps identify the line of reflection:

  • Mirror Images of Numbers: Some numbers or digits (from 0 to 9) look exactly the same in their mirror image. For example, the number 181 has a symmetrical mirror image, whereas 686 does not look the same unless reflected along a specific line.
  • Mirror Images of Letters and Words: Words like WOW can look the same in a mirror. Other words like CAT, TAP, HEN, and CAN can be read correctly by placing a mirror at specific positions.
  • Ambulance Lettering: The word "AMBULANCE" is written laterally inverted (backward) on the front of the vehicle so that drivers ahead can read it correctly in their rearview mirrors.

11.4.3 Analyzing Shapes

Different geometric shapes have different numbers of sides and lines of symmetry. Tracing and folding shapes is a practical method to find all their lines of symmetry.

11.5 Tiling and Tessellations

11.5.1 Tiling the Tiles

A tiling pattern (or tessellation) is created by repeating a basic unit (tile) to cover a flat surface without any gaps or overlaps. Tiles can be slid, flipped, or rotated to create different designs.

11.5.2 Designing Creative Tiles

We can design custom tiles using hexagonal or square grids. Joining two or more shapes can create new tiles that fit together perfectly.

11.5.3 Making a Catty Wall

A fun way to create a tiling pattern is the "cut, slide, and paste" method:

  • Step 1: Take a square piece of paper and mark a shape (like a cat's head and ears) at the top.
  • Step 2: Cut along the outline and slide the cut piece down to the bottom edge.
  • Step 3: Tape the pieces together to form a new tile.
  • Step 4: Trace this tile repeatedly on a larger sheet to create a seamless wall pattern with no gaps or overlaps.
11.6 Project: Nature Walk

Symmetry is abundant in nature. During a nature walk, we can observe and collect symmetrical items:

  • Leaves and flowers often exhibit clear lines of symmetry.
  • Butterflies are highly symmetrical.
  • Project Activities: Categorizing leaves into symmetrical and non-symmetrical, creating patterns/designs with leaves and flowers, and making greeting cards using leaf imprints.
