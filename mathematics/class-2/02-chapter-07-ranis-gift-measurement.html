Chapter 7: Rani's Gift

7.1 Rani's Gift: The Concept of Measurement

This section introduces the concept of measuring length using non-standard units like the handspan and explains why standard measurements are necessary.

The Story of Raja Jagdeep's Bed

Raja Jagdeep wanted to make a beautiful carved bed for his queen. He requested a bed that was $10$ handspans long.

  • The carpenter measured $10$ handspans using his own hand and built the bed.
  • When the queen tried to lie down, she could not fit because the bed was too short.
  • When the King measured the bed, it was only $8$ handspans long by his measurement.
  • When the young apprentice measured it, it was $14$ handspans long.

Key Lesson: A handspan is a non-standard unit of measurement. Because different people have different hand sizes, measuring with handspans leads to inconsistent results.

Let us Do: Estimating and Measuring Length

Practice measuring everyday classroom objects using your textbook or handspan to compare estimated length versus actual length:

  • Desk: Estimate the length and then measure the actual length.
  • Blackboard: Estimate and measure the actual length.
  • Window: Estimate and measure the actual length.
7.2 Comparing Paths and Heights

This section explores comparing lengths, finding the shortest/longest paths, and measuring height using uniform non-standard units.

Finding the Shortest and Longest Paths

When traveling from one point to another, different routes have different lengths:

  • Kavita's Path to School: Choosing the straightest, most direct path helps reach the destination fastest (shortest path).
  • Grid Paths: Comparing different paths from point $A$ to point $B$ on a grid to identify the longest and shortest routes. You can verify the length of these paths using a piece of thread.

Measuring Height with Blocks

Using uniform objects like blocks allows us to measure and compare heights accurately.

  • The height of different toy animals can be measured by stacking blocks next to them:
  • Giraffe: Measured in blocks tall.
  • Monkey: Measured in blocks tall.
  • Elephant: Measured in blocks tall.
7.3 Understanding Weight: Heavier and Lighter

This section introduces the concepts of weight, comparing objects to see which is heavier or lighter, and using a balance scale.

Pumpkin's Chaupal

Vegetables can be compared based on their weight:

  • Terms used: heavier, lighter, heaviest, and lightest.
  • A pumpkin is heavier than a watermelon, while a capsicum is lighter than a cauliflower.

Using a Balance Scale

A balance scale is used to compare the weights of two objects.

  • The side of the balance with the heavier object goes down.
  • The side of the balance with the lighter object goes up.
  • You can make your own simple balance scale using a clothes hanger, thread, and two paper cups.
7.4 Understanding Capacity: Volume and Liquid Measurement

This section introduces capacity, which is the amount of liquid a container can hold.

Making Nimbu Pani (Scaling Quantities)

To make $1$ glass of nimbu pani, the following ingredients are needed:

  • Water: $1$ glass
  • Sugar: $1$ spoon
  • Lemon juice: $2$ spoons
  • Salt: $1$ pinch

To make $3$ glasses of nimbu pani for friends, we must scale the ingredients by multiplying by $3$:

  • Water: $1 imes 3 = 3$ glasses
  • Sugar: $1 imes 3 = 3$ spoons
  • Lemon juice: $2 imes 3 = 6$ spoons
  • Salt: $1 imes 3 = 3$ pinches

Measuring Bottle Capacity

Different bottles hold different amounts of water. We can measure their capacity using a standard cup or glass:

  • Find out how many glasses of water are needed to completely fill your bottle versus your friend's bottle.
  • Jal Tarang: An instrument made by filling $5$ to $7$ similar glasses with varying levels of water. Tapping them with a spoon produces different musical sounds based on the water level.
