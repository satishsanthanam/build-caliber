10.2 How Much Rupal Spent?

Let's calculate the money spent by Rupal on different rides:

  • Merry-go-round: $\text{вВє}10$
  • Toy Train: $\text{вВє}20$
  • Giant Wheel: $\text{вВє}15$

Total money spent on rides: $\text{вВє}10 + \text{вВє}20 + \text{вВє}15 = \text{вВє}45$

Calculating Money Left

Using the formula:

$$\text{Money given by mother} - \text{Money spent} = \text{Money left}$$

$$\text{вВє}50 - \text{вВє}45 = \text{вВє}5$$

Alternative Way of Finding Money Spent

A friend of Rupal has $\text{вВє}8$ left out of $\text{вВє}50$. She wants to know how much money she spent. Her method of counting up:

  • $\text{вВє}8 + \text{вВє}20 = \text{вВє}28$ (spent on rides)
  • $\text{вВє}28 + \text{вВє}20 = \text{вВє}48$ (spent on snacks)
  • $\text{вВє}48 + \text{вВє}2 = \text{вВє}50$ (spent on candies)
  • Total spent: $\text{вВє}20 + \text{вВє}20 + \text{вВє}2 = \text{вВє}42$

Practice Problem

If you had $\text{вВє}3$ left when your father gave you $\text{вВє}40$ for the fair, how many rupees did you spend?

$$\text{вВє}40 - \text{вВє}3 = \text{вВє}37$$

10.4 Old Coins and Paise

In the past, smaller denominations of currency called Paise were used in India:

  • $10\text{ Paise}$
  • $20\text{ Paise}$
  • $25\text{ Paise}$
  • $50\text{ Paise}$

Conversion Rate:

$$100\text{ Paise} = 1\text{ Rupee } (\text{вВє}1)$$

$$50\text{ Paise} + 50\text{ Paise} = 100\text{ Paise} = \text{вВє}1$$

Practice Questions

  • How many 25 paise coins make вВє1?
    Four $25\text{ paise}$ coins make $\text{вВє}1$ ($4 \times 25 = 100\text{ paise}$).
  • How many 20 paise coins make вВє1?
    Five $20\text{ paise}$ coins make $\text{вВє}1$ ($5 \times 20 = 100\text{ paise}$).
