Chapter 10: Fun at the Fair

10.1 Introduction: Rupal at the Fair

Rupal loves going to the fair. She visits the local fair with her friends. Her mother gave her ‚āĻ50. She spent most of it on her favourite rides.

Discussion Topic: Talk about the things that children enjoy in fairs, whom they go with, what they buy, and how much money they spend there.

10.2 How Much Rupal Spent?

Let's calculate the money spent by Rupal on different rides:

  • Merry-go-round: $\text{‚āĻ}10$
  • Toy Train: $\text{‚āĻ}20$
  • Giant Wheel: $\text{‚āĻ}15$

Total money spent on rides: $\text{‚āĻ}10 + \text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}15 = \text{‚āĻ}45$

Calculating Money Left

Using the formula:

$$\text{Money given by mother} - \text{Money spent} = \text{Money left}$$

$$\text{‚āĻ}50 - \text{‚āĻ}45 = \text{‚āĻ}5$$

Alternative Way of Finding Money Spent

A friend of Rupal has $\text{‚āĻ}8$ left out of $\text{‚āĻ}50$. She wants to know how much money she spent. Her method of counting up:

  • $\text{‚āĻ}8 + \text{‚āĻ}20 = \text{‚āĻ}28$ (spent on rides)
  • $\text{‚āĻ}28 + \text{‚āĻ}20 = \text{‚āĻ}48$ (spent on snacks)
  • $\text{‚āĻ}48 + \text{‚āĻ}2 = \text{‚āĻ}50$ (spent on candies)
  • Total spent: $\text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}2 = \text{‚āĻ}42$

Practice Problem

If you had $\text{‚āĻ}3$ left when your father gave you $\text{‚āĻ}40$ for the fair, how many rupees did you spend?

$$\text{‚āĻ}40 - \text{‚āĻ}3 = \text{‚āĻ}37$$

10.3 Let us Do: Ride Tickets and Indian Currency

A. How much money will be needed to buy?

  • Toy Train Ride: $\text{‚āĻ}20$ per ticket. For $2$ tickets: $2 \times \text{‚āĻ}20 = \text{‚āĻ}40$
  • Merry-go-round: $\text{‚āĻ}10$ per ticket. If total money is $\text{‚āĻ}30$, number of tickets: $\frac{\text{‚āĻ}30}{\text{‚āĻ}10} = 3$ tickets
  • Giant Wheel: $\text{‚āĻ}15$ per ticket. For $2$ tickets: $2 \times \text{‚āĻ}15 = \text{‚āĻ}30$

B. Indian Currency Notes

Commonly used Indian currency notes include:

  • ‚āĻ100 note
  • ‚āĻ200 note
  • ‚āĻ500 note

C. Observing a ‚āĻ500 Note

  • Words: "Five hundred rupees" is written in words on the note.
  • Numbers: "500" is written in numbers multiple times on both sides.
  • Monument: The picture of the Red Fort (Lal Qila) is printed on the back of the $\text{‚āĻ}500$ note.
10.4 Old Coins and Paise

In the past, smaller denominations of currency called Paise were used in India:

  • $10\text{ Paise}$
  • $20\text{ Paise}$
  • $25\text{ Paise}$
  • $50\text{ Paise}$

Conversion Rate:

$$100\text{ Paise} = 1\text{ Rupee } (\text{‚āĻ}1)$$

$$50\text{ Paise} + 50\text{ Paise} = 100\text{ Paise} = \text{‚āĻ}1$$

Practice Questions

  • How many 25 paise coins make ‚āĻ1?
    Four $25\text{ paise}$ coins make $\text{‚āĻ}1$ ($4 \times 25 = 100\text{ paise}$).
  • How many 20 paise coins make ‚āĻ1?
    Five $20\text{ paise}$ coins make $\text{‚āĻ}1$ ($5 \times 20 = 100\text{ paise}$).
10.5 Pop the Balloon Game

Matru comes across a 'Pop the balloon' game. He gets $3$ chances for $\text{‚āĻ}10$. The prize money for popping different colored balloons is:

  • Pink Balloon: $\text{‚āĻ}1$
  • Red Balloon: $\text{‚āĻ}2$
  • Blue Balloon: $\text{‚āĻ}5$
  • Yellow Balloon: $\text{‚āĻ}10$
  • Green Balloon: $\text{‚āĻ}20$

A. Calculating Prize Money

  • Red + Pink: $\text{‚āĻ}2 + \text{‚āĻ}1 = \text{‚āĻ}3$
  • Red + Green + Red: $\text{‚āĻ}2 + \text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}2 = \text{‚āĻ}24$
  • Blue + Blue + Pink: $\text{‚āĻ}5 + \text{‚āĻ}5 + \text{‚āĻ}1 = \text{‚āĻ}11$
  • Green + Yellow: $\text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}10 = \text{‚āĻ}30$

B. Target Amounts

Which balloons to pop to get the following amounts?

  • ‚āĻ17: Yellow ($\text{‚āĻ}10$) + Blue ($\text{‚āĻ}5$) + Red ($\text{‚āĻ}2$)
  • ‚āĻ22: Green ($\text{‚āĻ}20$) + Red ($\text{‚āĻ}2$)
  • ‚āĻ31: Green ($\text{‚āĻ}20$) + Yellow ($\text{‚āĻ}10$) + Pink ($\text{‚āĻ}1$)

C. Highest Amount with 3 Balloons

To get the highest amount with 3 differently coloured balloons, target:

  • Green ($\text{‚āĻ}20$) + Yellow ($\text{‚āĻ}10$) + Blue ($\text{‚āĻ}5$)
  • Total amount won: $\text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}10 + \text{‚āĻ}5 = \text{‚āĻ}35$

D. Highest Amount with 4 Balloons

To get the highest amount with 4 differently coloured balloons, target:

  • Green ($\text{‚āĻ}20$) + Yellow ($\text{‚āĻ}10$) + Blue ($\text{‚āĻ}5$) + Red ($\text{‚āĻ}2$)
  • Total amount won: $\text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}10 + \text{‚āĻ}5 + \text{‚āĻ}2 = \text{‚āĻ}37$

Making Combinations for ‚āĻ85

10.7 Let us Buy: Shopping Calculations

Calculate the total cost of items, compare with the money you have, and find the money left or more money needed:

  • Item 1: Notebook ($\text{‚āĻ}15$) + Curd ($\text{‚āĻ}28$)
    • Money needed: $\text{‚āĻ}15 + \text{‚āĻ}28 = \text{‚āĻ}43$
    • Money you have: One $\text{‚āĻ}20$ note + One $\text{‚āĻ}2$ coin = $\text{‚āĻ}22$
    • More money needed: $\text{‚āĻ}43 - \text{‚āĻ}22 = \text{‚āĻ}21$
  • Item 2: Oranges ($\text{‚āĻ}30$) + Mug ($\text{‚āĻ}10$)
    • Money needed: $\text{‚āĻ}30 + \text{‚āĻ}10 = \text{‚āĻ}40$
    • Money you have: One $\text{‚āĻ}50$ note + One $\text{‚āĻ}5$ coin = $\text{‚āĻ}55$
    • Money left: $\text{‚āĻ}55 - \text{‚āĻ}40 = \text{‚āĻ}15$
  • Item 3: Crayons ($\text{‚āĻ}20$) + Water Bottle ($\text{‚āĻ}55$) + Ball ($\text{‚āĻ}20$)
    • Money needed: $\text{‚āĻ}20 + \text{‚āĻ}55 + \text{‚āĻ}20 = \text{‚āĻ}95$
    • Money you have: One $\text{‚āĻ}20$ note + Two $\text{‚āĻ}2$ coins = $\text{‚āĻ}24$
    • More money needed: $\text{‚āĻ}95 - \text{‚āĻ}24 = \text{‚āĻ}71$

Word Problems

  • Problem A: Jayant spent $\text{‚āĻ}25$ on pens and $\text{‚āĻ}40$ on notebooks. How much money did he spend in total?
    $$\text{‚āĻ}25 + \text{‚āĻ}40 = \text{‚āĻ}65$$

  • Problem C: A packet of bread costs $\text{‚āĻ}30$ and a chocolate costs $\text{‚āĻ}60$. How much money does Vivek need to buy these items?

  • Problem D: Ajay has $\text{‚āĻ}58$ with him. He spent $\text{‚āĻ}48$ in the market. How much money is left with him now?
    $$\text{‚āĻ}58 - \text{‚āĻ}48 = \text{‚āĻ}10$$
