Chapter 5: Playing with Lines

5.1 Introduction to Lines through Yoga (Aasanas)

Yoga poses (aasanas) can help us understand different types of lines formed by our body postures.

Let us Do: Identifying Lines in Aasanas

  • Vertical / Standing Lines: Look for straight upright postures.
  • Slanting Lines: Look for postures where arms or legs are tilted.
  • Curved Lines: Look for postures where the back or limbs are bent in a curve.
  • Horizontal / Sleeping Lines: Look for postures parallel to the ground.

Teacher's Note: Facilitate children in practicing yoga poses. Give instructions like keeping the back straight, arms straight, curving the back, or slanting the arms and legs. Introduce them to Ashtanga Yoga as described in the ancient Yoga Sutras of Patanjali.

5.2 What is Straight?

We can understand different types of lines by holding a piece of thread in our hands:

  • Sleeping / Horizontal Line: Formed when holding a thread straight and tight horizontally.
  • Slanting Line: Formed when holding a thread tight at an angle.
  • Standing / Vertical Line: Formed when holding a thread tight vertically.
  • Curved Line: Formed when bringing hands closer, letting the thread loose and curved.

Let us Do: Tracing Lines

Practice tracing missing vertical, horizontal, slanting, and curved lines in everyday pictures like playground equipment, swings, slides, and ladders.

5.3 Let us Play with Dots

Using grids of dots, we can create various geometric shapes and artistic figures.

A. Making Shapes with Straight Lines

Connect dots using only straight lines (vertical, horizontal, and slanting) to make shapes like squares, rectangles, triangles, and 3D boxes.

B. Drawing Figures with Curved Lines

Connect dots using curved lines to draw organic shapes like flowers, clouds, rainbows, and waves.

5.4 Creative Drawing with Lines

Combine different types of lines to create beautiful designs and patterns.

  • Design with two types of lines: e.g., combining only horizontal and vertical lines.
  • Design with three types of lines: e.g., combining horizontal, vertical, and slanting lines.
  • Design using all types of lines: Combining horizontal, vertical, slanting, and curved lines.
5.5 Project Work: Fun with Folding

Paper folding is an excellent way to explore lines and creases.

  • Folding a paper in half creates a straight crease in the center.
  • Repeated folding creates multiple creases representing different types of lines.
  • Activity A: Fold a paper with a friend to see who can get the maximum number of creases. Trace straight lines with a red crayon and slanting lines with a blue crayon.
  • Activity B: Try to make curved lines by paper folding.

Art Integration: Discuss traditional Indian art forms like Madhubani, Kalamkari, and Warli, which extensively use basic lines and shapes.
