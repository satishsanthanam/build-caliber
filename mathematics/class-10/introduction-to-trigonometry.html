Chapter 8: Introduction to Trigonometry

8.1 Trigonometric Ratios

Definitions and Core Concepts

  • Trigonometry: The study of relationships between the sides and angles of a triangle. Derived from the Greek words tri (three), gon (sides), and metron (measure).
  • Trigonometric Ratios: For a right-angled triangle ABC (right-angled at B):
    • sine of ∠A (sin A) = side opposite to ∠A / hypotenuse = BC / AC
    • cosine of ∠A (cos A) = side adjacent to ∠A / hypotenuse = AB / AC
    • tangent of ∠A (tan A) = side opposite to ∠A / side adjacent to ∠A = BC / AB = sin A / cos A
    • cosecant of ∠A (cosec A) = 1 / sin A = AC / BC
    • secant of ∠A (sec A) = 1 / cos A = AC / AB
    • cotangent of ∠A (cot A) = 1 / tan A = AB / BC = cos A / sin A
  • Key Properties:
    • The values of the trigonometric ratios of an angle do not vary with the lengths of the sides of the triangle, if the angle remains the same.
    • Since the hypotenuse is the longest side in a right triangle, the value of sin A or cos A is always less than or equal to 1.
    • Notation: (sin A)2 is written as sin2A. Note that (sin A)-1 = 1 / sin A ≠ sin-1A (which represents the inverse sine function).

8.3 Trigonometric Identities

Fundamental Identities

  • Identity 1: sin2 A + cos2 A = 1 (for 0° ≤ A ≤ 90°)
  • Identity 2: 1 + tan2 A = sec2 A (for 0° ≤ A < 90°)
  • Identity 3: cot2 A + 1 = cosec2 A (for 0° < A ≤ 90°)
