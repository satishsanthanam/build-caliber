Chapter 14: Probability

14.1 Probability — A Theoretical Approach

Core Concepts & Definitions

  • Equally Likely Outcomes: Outcomes are equally likely if there is no reason to prefer one over another (e.g., a fair coin landing heads or tails, or a fair die showing any number from 1 to 6).
  • Unbiased: A property of an experiment (like tossing a coin or rolling a die) where no external bias or interference affects the outcome.
  • Elementary Event: An event that has only one outcome of the experiment. The sum of the probabilities of all elementary events of an experiment is always 1.
  • Complementary Events: For any event E, the event "not E" (denoted as E̅) is its complement. Their relationship is defined by:
    P(E) + P(E̅) = 1 or P(E̅) = 1 - P(E).
  • Impossible Event: An event that cannot occur. Its probability is always 0.
  • Sure / Certain Event: An event that is guaranteed to occur. Its probability is always 1.
  • Range of Probability: The probability of any event E is a real number between 0 and 1 inclusive:
    0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1.

Probability Formulas

  • Empirical (Experimental) Probability: Based on actual trials and observations.
    P(E) = (Number of trials in which the event happened) / (Total number of trials)
  • Theoretical (Classical) Probability: Based on assumptions of equally likely outcomes.
    P(E) = (Number of outcomes favourable to E) / (Number of all possible outcomes of the experiment)
14.2 Common Probability Scenarios

1. Tossing Coins

  • Single Coin: Possible outcomes = {H, T} (Total = 2).
    P(H) = 1/2, P(T) = 1/2.
  • Two Coins: Possible outcomes = {(H,H), (H,T), (T,H), (T,T)} (Total = 4).
    P(at least one head) = 3/4.
  • Three Coins: Total outcomes = 23 = 8.

2. Throwing Dice

  • Single Die: Possible outcomes = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6} (Total = 6).
  • Two Dice: Total outcomes = 6 × 6 = 36.
    The outcomes can be represented as ordered pairs (x, y) where x is the score on the first die and y is the score on the second.

3. Playing Cards (52-Card Deck)

  • Total cards = 52, divided into 4 suits of 13 cards each:
    • Spades (♠) & Clubs (♣): Black colour (26 cards total)
    • Hearts (♥) & Diamonds (♦): Red colour (26 cards total)
  • Each suit contains: Ace, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, Jack (J), Queen (Q), King (K).
  • Face Cards: Kings, Queens, and Jacks (Total = 12 cards in a deck).

4. Geometric Probability

  • Used when outcomes are infinite (e.g., points on a line segment or region).
  • Formula: P(E) = (Measure of favourable region) / (Measure of total region)
    Example: Area of target region / Total area.
14.3 Chapter Summary
  • The theoretical probability of an event E is P(E) = Outcomes favourable to E / Total possible outcomes.
  • 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1.
  • P(E) + P(E̅) = 1.
  • The sum of probabilities of all elementary events is 1.
