Chapter 11: Areas Related to Circles

1. Core Definitions

  • Sector of a circle: The portion of the circular region enclosed by two radii and the corresponding arc.
    • Minor Sector: Sector with angle < 180°.
    • Major Sector: Sector with angle 360° - θ.
  • Segment of a circle: The portion of the circular region enclosed between a chord and the corresponding arc.
    • Minor Segment: Formed by the minor arc.
    • Major Segment: Formed by the major arc.
  • Remark: Unless stated otherwise, segment and sector refer to the minor segment and minor sector.

2. Essential Formulas

  • Length of an arc of a sector (angle θ, radius r): (θ / 360) × 2πr
  • Area of a sector (angle θ, radius r): (θ / 360) × πr²
  • Area of a segment: Area of the corresponding sector - Area of the corresponding triangle

3. Key Examples

Example 1: Area of Sector

Given: Radius r = 4 cm, angle θ = 30°, π = 3.14.

  • Area of minor sector: (30/360) × 3.14 × 4² ≈ 4.19 cm²
  • Area of major sector: πr² - Area of minor sector = (3.14 × 16) - 4.19 ≈ 46.1 cm²
Example 2: Area of Segment

Given: Radius r = 21 cm, angle θ = 120°, π = 22/7.

  • Area of sector: (120/360) × (22/7) × 21² = 462 cm²
  • Area of triangle: Uses trigonometry to find base and height. Area = (441/4)√3 cm²
  • Area of segment: 462 - (441/4)√3 cm²

4. Exercise 11.1 Applications

Common problem types involving sector and segment areas:

  • Calculating area swept by a clock's minute hand over a specific time.
  • Finding grazing area for tethered animals (e.g., horse tied to a square field corner).
  • Calculating material areas for symmetrical objects (e.g., brooches, umbrellas, round table covers).
  • Determining coverage area for sweeping mechanisms (e.g., car wipers, lighthouse beams).
