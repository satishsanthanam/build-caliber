Chapter 4: Exploring Some Geometric Themes

4.1 Fractals

  • Fractals: Self-similar shapes exhibiting the same or similar pattern over and over at smaller scales. Examples in nature include ferns, trees, and coastlines.
  • Sierpinski Carpet: Created by dividing a square into 9 smaller squares and removing the central one. Remaining squares at step n: R_n = 8^n.
  • Sierpinski Gasket: Created by dividing an equilateral triangle into 4 identical triangles and removing the central one.
  • Koch Snowflake: Created by dividing equilateral triangle sides into 3 parts and replacing the middle part with an outward bump.

4.2 Visualising Solids

Faces, Edges, and Vertices
  • Faces: Flat/plane surfaces forming the boundary of a solid.
  • Edges: Line segments forming the sides of the faces.
  • Vertices: Points where edges meet.
Prisms and Pyramids
  • Prism: Has two congruent polygons as opposite faces; all other faces are parallelograms.
  • Pyramid: Has a polygonal base and a point outside it connected by edges to the base vertices. A triangular pyramid is a Tetrahedron.
Nets
  • Net: A 2D shape obtained by unfolding a 3D solid onto a plane.
  • Cube: 11 possible nets.
  • Regular Tetrahedron: 2 possible nets.
  • Octahedron: 11 possible nets.
  • Dodecahedron: 43,380 possible nets.
Shortest Paths on a Solid
  • The shortest path between two points on a solid's surface is found by unfolding the solid into a net and drawing a straight line between the points.
  • The specific unfolding method determines the correct shortest path.

Representation of Solids on a Plane Surface

  • Projection: A 2D representation formed by lines perpendicular to a plane.
  • Front view: Projection on the vertical plane.
  • Top view: Projection on the horizontal plane.
  • Side view: Projection on the side plane.
  • Shadows cast by perpendicular light (like the Sun) are indistinguishable from projections. Parallel lines always project to parallel lines.

Isometric Projections

  • Isometric Projection: An orientation of a cube where the projections of all edges are of equal length.
  • Isometric Grid: A plane tiled with hexagons, used for drawing 3D objects.
  • Drawing relies on three primary axes: depth, length, and height.
