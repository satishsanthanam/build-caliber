Class 9 — Chapter 6: Measuring Space: Perimeter and Area

6.1 Perimeter of a Shape

Perimeter Formulas
  • perimeter is the total length around the border of a closed two-dimensional shape.
  • Square: $P = 4a$ (where $a$ is the side length). The ratio of perimeter to side is always $4:1$.
  • Equilateral Triangle: $P = 3a$ (where $a$ is the side length). The ratio of perimeter to side is always $3:1$.
  • Rectangle: $P = 2(a + b)$ (where $a$ is length and $b$ is width).
6.2 Perimeter of a Circle & The C/D Ratio

The Constant Ratio Pi (π)
  • The ratio of the circumference ($C$) to the diameter ($D$) of any circle is constant and is denoted by the Greek letter π (pi).
  • irrational number: $ u$ is irrational, meaning its decimal expansion goes on forever without repeating.
  • Historical Approximations:
    • Mesopotamia (c. 1900 BCE): $ u o 3.125$
    • Archimedes (c. 250 BCE): $3 rac{10}{71} < u < 3 rac{1}{7}$
    • Zu Chongzhi (480 CE): $ u o rac{355}{113} o 3.1415929$
    • Aryabhata (499 CE): $ u o rac{62832}{20000} = 3.1416$
    • Madhava of Sangamagrama: First exact infinite series: $ rac{ u}{4} = 1 - rac{1}{3} + rac{1}{5} - rac{1}{7} + ext{...}$
6.4 Length of an Arc of a Circle

Arc Length Formulas
  • Circumference: $C = 2 u r$ (where $r$ is the radius).
  • Semicircle Arc: $l = u r$
  • Quarter Circle Arc: $l = rac{ u r}{2}$
  • General Arc Length: For an arc subtending an angle $ heta^ heta$ at the center: $$l = 2 u r imes rac{ heta^ heta}{360^ heta}$$
6.6 & 6.7 Area of Rectangles and Parallelograms

Area Formulas
  • Rectangle: $A = ab$ (where $a$ and $b$ are adjacent sides).
  • Parallelogram: $A = ext{base} imes ext{height} = bh$. Any parallelogram can be geometrically transformed into a rectangle of equal base and height.
6.8 Area of a Triangle

Standard and Advanced Formulas
  • Standard Formula: $A = rac{1}{2} imes ext{base} imes ext{height} = rac{1}{2}bh$.
  • Median Property: A median of a triangle divides it into two triangles of equal area.
  • Heron's Formula: For a triangle with sides $a$, $b$, and $c$: $$A = ext{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$$ where $s = rac{a+b+c}{2}$ is the semi-perimeter.
  • Circumcircle Connection: $A = rac{abc}{4R}$ (where $R$ is the circumradius).
  • Incircle Connection: $A = r imes s$ (where $r$ is the incircle radius and $s$ is the semi-perimeter).
6.9 Brahmagupta's Formula for Cyclic 4-gons

Area of a Cyclic Quadrilateral
  • A cyclic 4-gon is a quadrilateral whose vertices all lie on a single circle.
  • Brahmagupta's Formula: $$A = ext{(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)(s-d)}$$ where $s = rac{a+b+c+d}{2}$ is the semi-perimeter.
  • Generalisation: Heron's formula is a special case of Brahmagupta's formula where one side length $d = 0$.
6.10 Area of a Circle & Sectors

Circular Regions
  • Area of a Circle: $A = u r^2$.
  • Sector of a Circle: The region bounded by an arc and two radii. Area of a sector with central angle $ heta^ heta$: $$A = u r^2 imes rac{ heta^ heta}{360^ heta}$$
  • Segment of a Circle: The region bounded by an arc and a chord.
