Class 7 — Chapter 1: Unit 1: Learning Together

Introduction: Learning Together

Unit 1 focuses on the theme of learning, perseverance, and appreciating our senses through three distinct literary pieces: The Day the River Spoke, Try Again, and Three Days to See.

Prose: The Day the River Spoke

Plot Summary

Jahnavi is a ten-year-old girl living in a coastal village in India. She dreams of going to school like her brothers, but is kept home to care for her younger siblings. One day, she hears the sleepy voice of the River, who encourages her to take charge of her destiny. Inspired, Jahnavi gathers courage to attend school, leading her teacher to visit her home and convince her parents to let her study.

Key Discussions

The dialogue between Jahnavi and the River highlights the contrast between her curiosity about nature (why spiders are yellow, why bamboo trees rustle) and her social limitations. The River acts as a mentor, reminding her that girls can do as much as boys and urging her to take the first step.

Poem: Try Again

Poem Summary

Written by Eliza Cook, this narrative poem tells the story of King Bruce of Scotland. Defeated and in despair, he watches a spider repeatedly fail but persist in climbing to its web. On its tenth attempt, the spider succeeds. Inspired by this display of perseverance, King Bruce resolves to try again and ultimately triumphs.

Literary Devices

  • Metaphor: The spider's struggle to reach its web is compared to King Bruce's struggle to free Scotland, highlighting the theme of perseverance.
  • Alliteration: Repetition of consonant sounds (e.g., 'cling and crawl', 'head grew steady').
  • Rhyme Scheme: The poem follows an ABAB rhyme scheme.

Vocabulary

  • endeavour: an attempt to do something new or difficult
  • toil: work very hard and/or for a long time
  • strive: to try very hard to achieve something
  • braced: prepared themselves for something difficult
Prose: Three Days to See

Summary

Helen Keller, who lost her sight and hearing in infancy, shares her perspective on how she experiences the world through touch. She outlines what she would do if granted sight for just three days, urging those who can see to appreciate their senses fully.

The Three-Day Plan

  • Day 1: See the people whose kindness and companionship made her life worth living.
  • Day 2: Greet the dawn and visit museums to see the history of the earth and the pageant of human progress.
  • Day 3: Spend time in the busy, workaday world, observing people going about their daily lives in the city.

Grammar & Language Study

Prepositions vs Adverbs

Words like on, off, up, and down can function as both prepositions and adverbs.

  • Preposition: Followed by an object (noun). Example: She ran up the stairs.
  • Adverb: Tells more about the action (verb). Example: She rang me up.

Modal Verbs

Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs expressing necessity, possibility, permission, or ability.

  • Permission: Can, Could, May
  • Obligation/Necessity: Must, Ought to
  • Advice: Should
  • Possibility: Might, May
  • Polite Request: Would, Could
