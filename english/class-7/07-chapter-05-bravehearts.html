Class 7 — Chapter 5: Bravehearts

A Homage to Our Brave Soldiers

This section features a correspondence between two friends, Soumya and Ananda, sharing their deep gratitude and reflections after visiting the National War Memorial in New Delhi.

The National War Memorial

  • Location & Size: Spread over 40 acres near the iconic India Gate in New Delhi.
  • Inauguration: Envisioned and inaugurated by Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi in February 2019.
  • Structure: Consists of four concentric circles representing the journey of a soldier:
    • Amar Chakra (Circle of Immortality): Houses the central 15-metre-tall obelisk with the eternal flame (Amar Jawan Jyoti).
    • Veerta Chakra (Circle of Bravery): A covered gallery exhibiting six bronze murals depicting valiant battle actions.
    • Tyag Chakra (Circle of Sacrifice): Circular concentric walls of honour covered with granite tablets bearing the names of over 26,000 fallen soldiers etched in golden letters.
    • Raksha Chakra (Circle of Protection): The outermost circle consisting of rows of trees representing soldiers guarding the nation's borders.

Gallantry Awards & Citations

The letters highlight the supreme sacrifices of India's bravehearts, recognized through prestigious gallantry awards:

  • Param Vir Chakra (PVC): India's highest wartime gallantry medal. Major Somnath Sharma was posthumously awarded India's first PVC in 1947.
  • Lance Naik Albert Ekka (PVC): Honoured for his exceptional bravery in the Battle of Gangasagar (1971), where he single-handedly silenced enemy machine-gun bunkers despite being mortally wounded.
  • Major Padmapani Acharya (MVC): Posthumously awarded the Maha Vir Chakra for his heroic leadership during the 1999 Kargil War, leading his platoon to capture a heavily fortified enemy position under intense fire.

Key Vocabulary

  • territorial integrity: Safeguarding a nation's borders.
  • envisioned: Visualised or conceptualised.
  • gallantry: Exceptional courage, especially in battle.
  • posthumously: Awarded or occurring after death.
  • citation: An official report praising a soldier's meritorious performance of duty.
  • obelisk: A tall, four-sided, tapering stone pillar.
  • wreaths: Circular arrangements of flowers used as a mark of respect and remembrance.
My Dear Soldiers (Poem)

A poignant tribute written by A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, celebrating the relentless dedication and sacrifices of Indian soldiers guarding the nation's borders.

Poetic Themes

  • Unwavering Vigilance: Soldiers guard the nation tirelessly while citizens sleep in peace.
  • Harsh Conditions: They endure extreme weather, from freezing snow to scorching desert heat.
  • Diverse Terrains: Their duties span mountains, valleys, deserts, marshes, seas, and the air.
  • Sacrifice of Youth: Giving the prime of their youth to secure the nation's future.

Literary Devices

  • Imagery: Vivid descriptions like "scorching sun's sweltering rays" and "lonely expanses".
  • Simile: "Treading the lonely expanses as yogis" (comparing soldiers' disciplined, solitary life to that of yogis).
  • Metaphor: "Wind chimes of my land vibrate your feat" (representing the nation's collective memory and gratitude).
  • Alliteration: Repetition of consonant sounds in "season or snowy" and "sun's sweltering".
Rani Abbakka

A graphic historical narrative of Rani Abbakka Chowta, the fearless 16th-century Queen of Ullal (Karnataka), who defied Portuguese colonial expansion.

Historical Narrative

  • Defiance of Colonial Power: Rani Abbakka refused to pay annual tribute to the Portuguese, declaring Ullal a sovereign country.
  • Courageous Leadership: Despite her husband (Veera Narasimha of Bangadi) and other local rulers warning her of Portuguese wrath, she stood firm and allied with the Zamorin of Kozhikode.
  • The Battle of Ullal: When the Portuguese attacked, she led her forces valiantly, launched counter-attacks on the Portuguese fort, and successfully defended her kingdom, driving the colonisers out.

Key Vocabulary

  • vassal: A state or ruler subordinate to a more powerful nation.
  • coronation: The ceremony of crowning a sovereign.
  • sovereign: Possessing supreme, independent authority.
  • dauntless: Fearless and determined.
  • avenge: To take vengeance or seek retribution for a wrong.
Grammar & Language Study

Direct and Indirect Speech

Rules for transforming direct speech into reported (indirect) speech:

  • Tense Shifts: Present simple shifts to past simple; present perfect shifts to past perfect; modals like 'will' shift to 'would'.
  • Pronoun Changes: Pronouns adjust to align with the reporter's perspective (e.g., "I" becomes "he" or "she").
  • Time and Place Expressions:
    • "now" becomes "then"
    • "today" becomes "that day"
    • "yesterday" becomes "the day before"
    • "here" becomes "there"

Prefixes & Collocations

  • Prefixes: Letters added to the beginning of a root word to change its meaning (e.g., dis- + regard = disregard; un- + daunted = undaunted).
  • Collocations: Natural combinations of words that frequently occur together (e.g., "barren landscape", "dense forest", "steady feet").
