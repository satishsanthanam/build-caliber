Class 1 — Chapter 5: A Farm

Poem: A Farm

A cheerful introductory poem about a grandfather's farm and the sounds made by different animals.

Stanza 1: The Cow

My dear grandpa had a farm
E-I-E-I-O
And on that farm,
He had a cow
E-I-E-I-O
With a moo-moo here
And a moo-moo there
Here a moo, there a moo
Everywhere a moo-moo
My dear grandpa had a farm
E-I-E-I-O

Stanza 2: The Hen

My dear grandpa had a farm
E-I-E-I-O
And on that farm,
He had a hen
E-I-E-I-O
With a cluck-cluck here
And a cluck-cluck there
Here a cluck, there a cluck
Everywhere a cluck-cluck
My dear grandpa had a farm
E-I-E-I-O

Activity: Animal Sounds

Continue the rhyme by replacing the animals with other farm animals and their sounds from your language (e.g., cat - miaon-miaon).

Let us read: Animals and Birds

A short story about a boy named Jaggu who lives in Nagpur and introduces the animals and birds living near his house.

Animals Near Jaggu's House

  • Dog: Lives near the gate.
  • Cats: Jump up and down in the verandah.
  • Cow: Eats grass.
  • Rat: Very small; chased by the cat.
  • Monkeys: Swing and jump in the trees.
  • Snakes: Live in the fields beside the house; one must walk carefully.

Birds Near Jaggu's House

  • Crow: Black in colour.
  • Pigeon: Grey in colour.
  • Sparrow: A small bird.
  • Parrot: Green with a red beak.
  • Peacock: Has many beautiful colours.
Vocabulary & Phonetics

New Words

  • verandah - an open, roofed porch or portico
  • cow - a fully grown female animal of a domesticated breed of ox
  • rat - a rodent resembling a large mouse
  • sparrow - a small brown and grey bird
  • crow - a large black bird with a harsh cry
  • pigeon - a stout-bodied bird with a small head
  • snake - a long limbless reptile
  • fields - areas of open land
  • peacock - a large male bird with brilliant blue and green plumage and a long tail
  • beak - a bird's horny projecting jaws

Phonetics: Ending Sounds

Focus on identifying the final sound of words:

  • The last sound of the word rat is t.
  • The last sound of the word cap is p.
  • The last sound of the word jam is m.
  • The last sound of the word pen is n.
  • The last sound of the word bell is l.
