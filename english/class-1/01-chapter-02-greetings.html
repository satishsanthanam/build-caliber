Class 1 — Chapter 2: Greetings

Daily Greetings

Learning how to greet others at different times of the day is an essential social skill. Here are the common greetings in English and Hindi:

  • When meeting someone: Namaste
  • In the morning: Good morning
  • In the afternoon: Good afternoon
  • In the evening: Good evening
  • Before going to bed: Good night
Rhymes and Songs

Hop a Little

A fun action rhyme to encourage movement and physical activity:

Hop a little, jump a little,
one two three.
Stamp a little, skip a little,
tap one knee.
Dance a little, twist a little,
shake your hand.
Yawn a little, sleep a little,
in your bed.

Key Action Words: hop, jump, stamp, skip, tap, dance, twist, shake, yawn, sleep.

My Family

A song celebrating family members and playing together:

Mother, mother, Will you play with me? Yes, I shall, yes, I shall, We are a family!
Father, father, Will you eat with me? Yes, I shall, yes, I shall, We are a family!
Brother, sister, Will you dance with me? Yes, I shall, yes, I shall, We are a family!
Grandmother, grandmother, Will you sing with me? Yes, I shall, yes, I shall, We are a family!
Grandfather, grandfather, Will you read with me? Yes, I shall, yes, I shall, We are a family!

Stories

The Sparrow Family

Summary: Mama and Papa sparrow build a soft, warm, and safe nest. Mama sparrow lays three small eggs. One day, the eggs hatch, and three little baby sparrows pop out. Papa sparrow brings food for them. Family members, including Grandmother, Grandfather, Aunt, Uncle, cousins, and friends, all visit to meet the new babies. As the baby sparrows grow bigger, they eventually hop out of the nest and fly high into the big blue sky.

  • New Vocabulary: nest, sparrow, egg, grandmother, grandfather, aunt, uncle, cousin, mother, father
  • Sight Words: and, they, them
Phonics & Letter Sounds

Alphabet Vocabulary (A-Z)

Practice initial letter sounds with these words:

  • f: fox, foot, fish
  • g: goat, gate, girl
  • h: house, hand, hut
  • i: iron, ice, inkpot
  • j: jacket, jackfruit, jug
  • k: kettle, kite, key
  • l: leaf, lion, lemon
  • m: mouth, mango, mouse
  • n: nine, nest, nose
  • o: owl, ox, orange
  • p: pencil, pot, parrot
  • q: question mark, quilt, quill
  • r: rainbow, rose, ring
  • s: spoon, swan, seven
  • t: ten, tomato, table
  • u: uniform, utensils
  • v: van, vase
  • w: well, wall
  • x: x-ray, xylophone
  • y: yak, yo-yo
  • z: zip, zebra

Initial Sound Focus: 'f' and 'm'

  • Words starting with 'f': father, fox, feather, food, foot, five, flag
  • Words starting with 'm': mother, man, mask, mouse, moon, monkey, mouth
Activities & Action Words

Action Words (Verbs)

Recognizing and practicing daily action words:

  • combing hair
  • seeing the teacher
  • eating food
  • hearing the bell
  • walking to school
  • playing football
  • running, stretching, sleeping, bending, skipping

Games & Crafts

  • Hopscotch: A traditional game where children take turns to jump and hop through numbered boxes (1 to 7) drawn on the floor.
  • Puppet Making: Creating family puppets using paper strips and hand-drawn faces of family members to encourage role-play.
