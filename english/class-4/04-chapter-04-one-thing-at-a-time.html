Class 4 — Chapter 4: One Thing at a Time

Poem: One Thing at a Time

The Poem

Stanza 1:
Work while you work,
Play while you play;
To be useful and happy,
That is the way.

Stanza 2:
All that you do,
Do with all your might;
Things done by halves,
Are never done right.

Stanza 3:
One thing at a time,
And that done well;
Is a very good rule,
As many can tell.

Stanza 4:
Moments should never,
Be trifled away;
So, work while you work,
And play while you play.

Key Themes

  • Focus and Dedication: Doing one task at a time with full concentration leads to better results.
  • Time Management: Separating work and play ensures both productivity and happiness.
  • Efficiency: Avoiding half-hearted efforts ("things done by halves") because they are never done correctly.
Vocabulary & Word Meanings
  • might: strength or power
  • useful: helpful or beneficial
  • moments: short periods of time
  • trifled away: wasted or spent on unimportant things
Phonics: Silent Letters

The Silent 'l'

In many English words, the letter l is silent and not pronounced. Examples from the chapter include:

  • halves
  • calm
  • calf
  • chalk
  • talk
  • balm
  • palm
Grammar & Writing

Present Continuous Tense

To describe ongoing actions, use the structure: is/are + action word + -ing.

  • Example 1: Two girls are going to school.
  • Example 2: A woman is typing on a computer.

Daily Routine & Time (a.m. / p.m.)

  • a.m. (Ante Meridiem): Used for time from midnight to before noon.
  • p.m. (Post Meridiem): Used for time from noon to before midnight.
