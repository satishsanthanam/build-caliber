Class 4 — Chapter 1: Together We Can

Poem: Together We Can

This poem highlights the power of teamwork, unity, and mutual support. It teaches us that when we work together, we can overcome any obstacle and share both joy and success.

Stanza Breakdown & Key Themes

  • Unity & Strength: Standing together makes us strong and prevents us from falling.
  • Teamwork: Working as a team helps us overcome any challenge (Teamwork can overcome anything).
  • Trust & Sharing: Win or lose, a team shares everything, built on a strong bond of trust.
  • Shared Joy: When we work with team spirit, our hearts shine as one, bringing true joy.
Let us Think & Speak

Discussion & Comprehension

  • Joining Hands: Working together brings us closer to our goals.
  • Importance of Helping: Supporting each other ensures collective success and builds trust.
  • Conflict Resolution: A team leader must listen to disagreeing teammates and find common ground to unite the team.

Unity in Diversity

Through the conversation of Bokali and Darsana, we learn that India has diverse traditions, food, festivals, and languages, yet we stand united (Unity in Diversity).

Let us Learn: Grammar & Vocabulary

Collective Nouns

Words used to describe a group of people, animals, or things are called collective nouns.

  • A bundle of sticks
  • A herd of sheep
  • A pack of wolves
  • A bouquet of flowers
  • A grove of trees
  • A swarm of bees
  • A choir of boys
  • A bunch of keys

Prepositions of Place

Words that show the position or location of something in relation to another object are called prepositions.

  • Behind: The post office is behind the hospital.
  • In front of: There is a bookstore in front of the Children's Park.
  • Near: The bank is near the school.
  • Between: There is an ambulance between the post office and the hospital.

Teamwork in Nature

  • Honeybees: Use a waggle dance to communicate the exact location of food and water sources to their hive members.
  • Ants: Work as a team using signals to find food, defend their colony, and build complex nests.
Let us Write: Future Tense

Using 'Will' for Future Events

We use the helper verb will to talk about actions or events that will take place in the future.

  • Example 1: What is Lubang doing to help his friend, and what will they do next?
  • Example 2: What are the students doing, and what will the teacher do next?
Let us Do: Togetherness Activity

Creating a Symbol of Togetherness

An activity to create a paper chain of human figures holding hands, symbolizing unity.

  1. Fold a sheet of paper multiple times.
  2. Draw one half of a human figure (head, neck, hand, dress, leg, shoe) on the folded edge.
  3. Cut along the drawing with scissors.
  4. Unfold the paper gently to reveal a chain of figures holding hands.
  5. Colour and decorate the chain.
