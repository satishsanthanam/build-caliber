Class 4 — Chapter 12: Maheshwar

Chapter Summary & Reading Passage

The chapter is written in the form of a dialogue between two children, Aarav and Ananya, and their Chachi (paternal aunt). They discuss the majestic Maheshwar Fort after seeing its picture in a newspaper.

The History of Maheshwar Fort

  • Builder: The fort was built long ago by Queen Ahilya Bai of the great Holkar family.
  • Location: It is situated on a hill on the banks of the Narmada River, about 95 kilometres away from Indore in Madhya Pradesh.
  • Purpose: Built as a fortress on a hill, it allowed the Queen to see her entire kingdom of Maheshwar. It is also known as Ahilya Fort.

Architectural Features

  • Overhanging Balconies: The fort is famous for its unique overhanging balconies that seem to float in the air.
  • Construction Materials: It was constructed using special red stones brought from Gujarat and Rajasthan.
  • Grand Staircases: The fort features a unique style of construction with grand staircases and beautifully designed temples nearby.
  • The Queen's Statue: A 13-feet tall statue of Queen Ahilya Bai stands in front of the palace, acting like a guardian watching over her land.

Maheshwari Sarees

Maheshwar is also famous for its handloom weaving tradition:

  • Materials: Originally made of pure silk, cotton yarn was later introduced. Today, they showcase a blend of cotton and silk threads.
  • Features: Known for their unique reversible borders and pallus.
  • Traditional Colours: Woven in vibrant colours like peacock blue, bright yellow, forest green, and an Indian red dye called Aal.
Vocabulary & Word Meanings

New Words

  • fort: A strong fortified building.
  • majestic: Having or showing impressive beauty or scale.
  • overhanging: Hanging or extending over something.
  • balconies: Platforms projecting from the wall of a building.
  • ruler: A person who rules a territory or state.
  • unique: Being the only one of its kind.
  • Chachi: Paternal aunt.

Synonyms & Word Groups

Words with similar meanings grouped together:

  • Smiling, grinning, laughing (expressions of happiness)
  • Happy, joyful, cheerful (states of joy)
  • Brave, bold, fearless (states of courage)
Grammar & Language Study

Silent Letters (Silent 'g')

In some English words, the letter 'g' is silent and not pronounced:

  • design (the 'g' is silent)
  • sign (the 'g' is silent)
  • high (the 'g' is silent)
  • light (the 'g' is silent)
  • night (the 'g' is silent)

Matching Adjectives to Meanings

  • Kindness: Being kind
  • Bravery: Being brave
  • Wisdom: Being wise
  • Majestic: Being royal
  • Unique: Being special
  • Fierce: Being strong
  • Dedicated: Being devoted

Articles (a, an, the)

Articles are used to point out nouns:

  • 'a' / 'an': Used for non-specific singular nouns (e.g., a monkey, an oak tree).
  • 'the': Used when referring to something specific or already mentioned (e.g., the naughty monkey, the bag).
Exercises & Activities

Comprehension Questions

  1. Who was Ahilya Bai?
  2. Where is Maheshwar Fort located? What makes its location special?
  3. What materials were used to build Maheshwar Fort, and where did they come from?
  4. What does the statue of Queen Ahilya Bai in front of the palace represent?

Creative Writing & Speaking

  • Speaking Project: Research and present a historical monument in your town or nearby, including its name, history, special features, and emotional connection to the people.
  • Writing Prompt A: Imagine visiting Maheshwar Fort with your family. Describe what you see and feel as you explore the balconies and temples.
  • Writing Prompt B: Write a short factual paragraph about Maheshwar Fort based on the lesson.
