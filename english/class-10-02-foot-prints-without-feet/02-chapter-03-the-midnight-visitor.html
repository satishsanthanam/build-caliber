Class 10 (footprints without feet) — Chapter 3: The Midnight Visitor

Chapter Overview & Characters

"The Midnight Visitor" by Robert Arthur is a suspenseful spy story that subverts traditional stereotypes of secret agents. It highlights how intelligence, presence of mind, and calm composure can triumph over physical threats and weapons.

Character Profiles

  • Ausable: A secret agent who does not fit the typical description of a spy. He is very fat, sloppy, speaks with an American accent, and lives in a small, gloomy room on the sixth floor. However, he possesses extraordinary presence of mind, wit, and quick thinking.
  • Fowler: A young, romantic writer who seeks authentic thrills for his writing. He is initially disappointed by Ausable's mundane appearance and lifestyle but witnesses a real-life thrill by the end of the evening.
  • Max: A slender, crafty rival secret agent who threatens Ausable with an automatic pistol to steal a highly confidential report on new missiles. He is easily deceived due to his nervousness and lack of critical thinking.
  • Henry: A waiter at the French hotel whose timely arrival with drinks is cleverly used by Ausable to trick Max.
Plot Summary

An Unpromising Beginning

Fowler accompanies Ausable to his gloomy room on the sixth floor of a French hotel. Fowler feels let down because Ausable is fat, sloppy, and lacks the mysterious, romantic aura of a fictional spy.

The Midnight Intruder

Upon entering the room and switching on the light, they find Max standing in the middle of the room with a small automatic pistol. Max demands the secret report on new missiles that Ausable is expecting that night.

The Balcony Deception

Instead of panicking, Ausable calmly sits down and fabricates a story about a non-existent balcony outside his window. He complains bitterly about the hotel management, claiming it is the second time someone has entered his room through that balcony from the adjacent apartment. Max, who entered using a passkey, believes this story completely.

The Knock at the Door

Suddenly, a loud knocking is heard at the door. Ausable claims it is the police, whom he had requested to check on him for the safety of the important report. Terrified of being caught or shot, Max panics.

The Fatal Leap

Max backs towards the window, swings his leg over the sill, and jumps down to hide on the "balcony," screaming shrilly as he falls to his death. The door opens to reveal Henry, the waiter, carrying the drinks Ausable had ordered. Ausable reveals to a stunned Fowler that there was never any police, and more importantly, there is no balcony.

Thematic Analysis & Key Concepts
  • Deception vs. Reality: The story contrasts the romanticized, cinematic depiction of spies with the realistic, mundane appearance of Ausable, proving that real espionage relies on intellect rather than physical glamour.
  • Presence of Mind: Ausable's ability to remain calm under gunpoint and instantly construct a flawless, convincing lie demonstrates that mental sharpness is far more powerful than physical force.
  • The Power of Suggestion: Max's downfall is caused by his own fear and unquestioning belief in Ausable's detailed description of the balcony.
Glossary & Vocabulary
  • romantic: imaginative; having a fantastic view of reality
  • passably: just well enough; tolerably well
  • espionage: spying
  • sloppy: carelessly dressed
  • prosaic: ordinary
  • chuckled: laughed quietly, without opening his mouth
  • wheezed: spoke breathing noisily and heavily
  • missiles: weapons directed by remote control or automatically
  • shrilly: piercingly; in a high pitch
