Class 10 (footprints without feet) — Chapter 8: Bholi

Chapter Overview

Bholi is a touching story by K.A. Abbas about Sulekha, a young girl who overcomes physical disfigurement, a speech impediment, and social neglect through the power of education and a compassionate teacher.

Character Analysis

Bholi (Sulekha)

  • Initial State: Nicknamed simpleton due to a childhood brain injury; physically scarred by smallpox and suffers from a severe stammer.
  • Transformation: Gains confidence through her teacher's kindness and education.
  • Final State: Emerges as a strong, independent woman who stands up for her self-respect and rejects a greedy marriage proposal.

The Teacher

  • A kind, patient, and encouraging educator who treats Bholi with affection and respect.
  • Acts as the catalyst for Bholi's transformation, turning her fear into hope and confidence.

Ramlal & His Wife

  • Ramlal is a prosperous village official (numberdar) who is deeply concerned about Bholi's marriage prospects due to her looks and lack of intellect.
  • His wife represents traditional, patriarchal attitudes, viewing daughters as burdens and dismissing Bholi's potential.
Plot Summary

Childhood and Neglect

Sulekha suffers a brain injury at ten months and smallpox at two years, leaving her physically and mentally challenged. Neglected by her family, she grows up isolated and silent, constantly mocked for her stammer.

The Turning Point: School

When a primary school opens in the village, Ramlal is pressured by the Tehsildar to send his daughters. Bholi is sent because her parents believe she has no future anyway. At school, a compassionate teacher encourages her to overcome her stammer and learn.

The Marriage Alliance and Climax

Years later, the family arranges Bholi's marriage to Bishamber, a wealthy but elderly and greedy grocer. Upon seeing Bholi's pockmarked face, Bishamber demands a hefty dowry of five thousand rupees. To save her father's honor (izzat), Ramlal pays, but Bholi courageously rejects the marriage, throwing the garland into the fire.

Resolution

Bholi speaks clearly and confidently, declaring she will not marry a greedy coward. She decides to dedicate her life to serving her parents in their old age and teaching at the school that empowered her.

Themes & Key Concepts
  • Empowerment through Education: Education is depicted as a liberating force that transforms a vulnerable girl into a self-reliant woman.
  • Gender Discrimination and Social Prejudices: The story highlights the unequal treatment of daughters and the societal obsession with physical appearance and dowry.
  • Self-Respect vs. Social Conformity: Bholi's ultimate refusal to marry Bishamber underscores the importance of individual dignity over regressive social expectations.
Glossary
  • simpleton: A foolish person easily tricked by others.
  • numberdar: An official who collects land revenue.
  • matted: Entangled or dirty hair.
  • squatted: Sat on one's heels.
  • scurried: Ran or moved hurriedly.
  • downcast: Looking downwards due to shame or sadness.
