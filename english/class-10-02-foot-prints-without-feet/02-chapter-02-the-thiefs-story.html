Class 10 (footprints without feet) — Chapter 2: The Thief's Story

Overview & Characters

The Thief's Story by Ruskin Bond is a touching narrative about a young thief's transformation through trust, love, and the promise of education.

Character Profiles

  • Hari Singh (The Narrator): A 15-year-old experienced and fairly successful thief. He changes his name every month to evade the police and former employers. He is cunning but ultimately redeemable.
  • Anil: A 25-year-old tall, lean, and easy-going writer. He is extremely trusting, kind, and simple, making him an unconventional employer.
Plot Summary

1. The Meeting and Deception

Hari Singh approaches Anil during a wrestling match, using flattery to win his confidence. He asks for work. Anil cannot pay him but agrees to feed him if Hari can cook. Hari lies that he can cook, but the first meal is so terrible that Anil gives it to a stray dog. Instead of throwing him out, Anil kindly teaches him to cook and write.

2. Life with Anil

Hari finds working for Anil pleasant. He makes tea in the morning and buys daily supplies, keeping a profit of about one rupee a day. Anil is aware of this minor theft but chooses to ignore it. Anil's income is irregular; he makes money by fits and starts, writing for magazines.

3. The Theft

One evening, Anil returns with a bundle of notes (600 rupees in fifties) from selling a book. He tucks the money under his mattress. Tempted by the large sum, Hari decides to steal it. He creeps up to the sleeping Anil, slides his hand under the mattress, and takes the money, planning to catch the 10.30 Express to Lucknow.

4. The Turning Point

At the railway station, Hari hesitates to board the train for an inexplicable reason and misses it. Standing alone on a deserted platform in a chilly rain, he reflects on his life. He realizes that while greedy men show fear and rich men show anger when robbed, a trusting man like Anil would only show sadness for the loss of trust. He also realizes that learning to read and write whole sentences is worth far more than a few hundred rupees.

5. Redemption

Hari returns to Anil's room and carefully slips the damp notes back under the mattress. The next morning, Anil wakes up early, makes tea, and hands Hari a wet fifty-rupee note. Anil announces that he made some money and will now pay Hari regularly, adding, 'Today we'll start writing sentences.' Anil clearly knows about the theft but chooses forgiveness over punishment, prompting a genuine, effortless smile from Hari.

Thematic Analysis

The Power of Trust and Kindness

Anil's unconditional trust and compassion act as a catalyst for Hari's reformation. Unlike the law or physical punishment, Anil's quiet forgiveness appeals to Hari's conscience, proving that love and trust can reform even a seasoned criminal.

The Value of Education

Hari realizes that physical wealth from stealing is temporary and risky, whereas education offers respect, status, and limitless opportunities. The desire to learn to read and write is what ultimately drives him back to Anil.

Glossary & Key Vocabulary
  • flattery: Insincere praise to win favor.
  • modestly: Without boasting; in a humble manner.
  • grunting: Making low, guttural sounds.
  • appealing: Attractive, inviting, or pleasing.
  • unlined: Showing no signs of worry, anxiety, or age.
  • fits and starts: Irregularly; not continuously.
Study Questions

Think About It

  1. What are Hari Singh's reactions to the prospect of receiving an education? Do they change over time?
  2. Why does Anil not hand the thief over to the police? In what ways is Anil different from other employers?

Talk About It

  1. Do you think people like Anil and Hari Singh are found only in fiction, or are there such people in real life?
  2. Does Anil's career as a struggling writer explain his behavior in any way?
