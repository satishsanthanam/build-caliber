Class 5 — Chapter 4: The Wise Parrot

Story Summary & Analysis

Plot Summary

In a quiet village, a rich merchant keeps a bright, talkative parrot in a golden cage. Although well-cared for, the parrot longs for freedom. When the merchant travels to a far-off land, the parrot asks him to deliver a message of greeting to its wild relatives in the forest. Upon hearing the message, one wild parrot drops seemingly dead to the ground. When the merchant returns and shares this distressing news, his pet parrot immediately mimics the action, falling to the floor of its cage. Believing it dead, the merchant places the bird on the windowsill, from where it happily flies away, having learned the trick of escape from its wise forest relative.

Key Themes

  • Freedom vs. Captivity: The story highlights that no matter how luxurious a cage is (even one made of gold), it cannot replace the joy of freedom.
  • Intelligence and Wisdom: True wisdom lies in understanding silent messages and using clever strategies rather than physical strength to achieve one's goals.
Vocabulary & Word Meanings
  • merchant: A person involved in wholesale trade or retaining goods.
  • perch: A bar or branch on which a bird aligns or sits.
  • tender: Soft, delicate, or easy to chew.
  • windowsill: A shelf along the bottom of a window.
  • distressed: Suffering from anxiety, sorrow, or pain.
  • sighed: Emitted a long, deep, audible breath expressing sadness or relief.

Present Perfect Tense

Used to talk about actions completed in the recent past or experiences that affect the present.

Rule: Subject + has/have + Verb (Past Participle) + Object

  • I have visited Delhi many times.
  • She has drawn pictures of ten monuments.
  • Mr. Joon has bought a new car.
  • I have completed my homework and my brother has revised for his test.
  • They have written letters to their friends.
