Class 5 — Chapter 10: Glass Bangles

Story Summary

The story is set in the busy district of Firozabad in Uttar Pradesh, famous for its rich tradition of glassware and beautiful glass bangles. Ravi, a skilled glass artisan, and his family have been crafting these bangles for generations. The process of making glass bangles is highly skilled and difficult, involving heating glass in a furnace, carefully shaping it, adding vibrant colors, and cooling it to perfection.

Ananya's Innovation

Ravi's daughter, Ananya, joins him after completing her schooling. She brings a fresh perspective, wanting to blend modern design elements with traditional craftsmanship. She introduces delicate floral patterns and geometric shapes. Though initially hesitant, Ravi supports her, and together they create a special collection for Diwali.

Community Impact

The new collection is a massive success. The increased demand for these innovative bangles not only brings prosperity to Ravi's workshop but also generates more employment for local artisans, packers, and transporters, positively impacting the entire community.

Vocabulary & New Words
  • clinking: A sharp, ringing sound made by glass or metal objects hitting each other.
  • artisans: Skilled workers who make things by hand.
  • furnace: An enclosed chamber in which heat is produced to melt glass or metal.
  • floral patterns: Designs featuring flowers and leaves.
  • elements: Parts or features of a design or style.
Grammar & Language Study

Past Perfect Tense

The past perfect tense is used to talk about an action that took place and was completed before another action in the past.

Rule: Subject + had + Past Participle (Verb 3) [for the first action] ... Simple Past [for the second action].

  • Example: Ananya had completed her schooling (First Action) before she joined the factory (Second Action).
  • Example: The crowd had dispersed (First Action) when we reached the market (Second Action).

Punctuation & Capitalization

Sentences must begin with capital letters, use proper nouns (like names of people and festivals) with capital letters, and end with appropriate punctuation marks like full stops (.) or question marks (?).

  • Incorrect: did ravi like ananya's new designs at first
  • Correct: Did Ravi like Ananya's new designs at first?

Financial Literacy: Savings Account

A Savings Account is a safe place in a bank to keep money, similar to a traditional gullak (piggy bank). It offers several benefits:

  • Meant for saving money securely.
  • Allows you to add or withdraw money at any time.
  • Earns interest as a reward for keeping your savings in the bank.
