Santoor Grade 5 — Unit 1

Chapter 1: Papa's Spectacles

Poem Overview

A lighthearted and humorous narrative poem about a father who loses his only pair of spectacles. He conducts a frantic and frantic search all over the house, turning rooms upside down, only to realize that they were resting safely on top of his own head the entire time.

Core Vocabulary & Literary Devices

spectacles search humorous frantic realize irony
Let us Think: Comprehension Q&A

Textual Analysis & Discussion Points

  1. What is the central conflict in the poem?
    Answer: The central conflict stems from Papa losing his spectacles and searching everywhere for them, unaware that they are on his head.
  2. Why is the ending considered humorous or ironic?
    Answer: It is ironic because after searching high and low throughout the entire house, the object of his search was physically attached to him the entire time.
