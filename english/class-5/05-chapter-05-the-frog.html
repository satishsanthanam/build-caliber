Class 5 — Chapter 5: The Frog

Poem: The Frog

Poem Recitation & Text

The poem describes the life, habits, and characteristics of a frog in a playful, first-person narrative.

Stanza 1 & 2 (Habitat & Origin):
I sit in ponds and in the rain,
Or hop about in a muddy drain.
As a tadpole, I swim with cheer,
A tiny fish-like thing, so clear!

Stanza 3 & 4 (Feeding & Adaptation):
I flick my tongue to catch my food,
It works so fast—now that’s quite good!
I live on land and water too,
In places damp with drops of dew.

Stanza 5 & 6 (Camouflage & Predators):
My colours match the plants and trees,
I hide so well in grass and leaves!
But snakes are near, I must beware,
Or else they’ll catch me unaware!

Stanza 7 & 8 (Sounds & Movement):
I croak my songs, they sound so loud,
Yet in my pond, I feel so proud.
I hop around, I jump so high,
Up I go into the sky!
With webbed feet, I swim with ease,
And leap so high in joyful breeze!

Key Themes

  • Amphibious Life: The frog lives both on land and in water, adapting to both environments.
  • Metamorphosis: Reference to its early life stage as a fish-like tadpole.
  • Survival & Camouflage: Using color-matching to hide from predators like snakes.
  • Physical Adaptations: Fast-flicking tongue for catching prey and webbed feet for swimming.
Vocabulary & Word Meanings
  • hop: To move by jumping on one or all feet.
  • muddy: Covered in or full of mud.
  • flick: A sudden quick movement (especially of the tongue).
  • damp: Slightly wet or moist.
  • dew: Tiny drops of water that form on cool surfaces at night.
  • beware: To be careful or alert to danger.
  • croak: The deep, hoarse sound made by a frog.
  • webbed: Having skin connecting the toes (helps in swimming).
Let us Think (Comprehension & Discussion)

A. Answer the following questions

  1. Where does the frog like to sit or spend time? The frog likes to sit in ponds, in the rain, in muddy drains, and in damp places with drops of dew.
  2. How does the frog catch its food? The frog catches its food by quickly flicking its fast-working tongue.
  3. How does the frog move in water and on land? The frog swims easily in water using its webbed feet, and hops or jumps high on land.
  4. What helps the frog hide in its surroundings? Its colors match the plants and trees, allowing it to hide well in grass and leaves.

B. Think and discuss

  • Living on land and water: Discussing the unique lifestyle of amphibians.
  • Hopping high: Creative imagination of what students would do if they could jump like frogs.
  • Frog vs. Fish: Comparing a frog's dual-life (amphibian) with a fish's purely aquatic life.
  • Webbed feet vs. Flippers: Understanding how human scuba divers mimic nature's design (biomimicry) using flippers.
Let us Learn (Grammar & Language)

A. Conjunctions (Linking Words)

Conjunctions are words used to connect clauses or sentences.

  • and: Used to join similar ideas. (e.g., "My colours match the plants and trees.")
  • or: Used to show choices. (e.g., "Would you like to learn about frogs or tadpoles?")
  • but: Used to show contrast or unexpected turns. (e.g., "I hop around happily, but I must watch out for snakes!")
  • because: Used to show reason. (e.g., "Frogs swim easily because they have webbed feet.")

B. Sentence Rearrangement

Rearranging jumbled words into meaningful sentences:

  • hunts/night/the/silently/tiger/at → The tiger hunts silently at night.
  • elephants/in/forests/live/large/groups/in → Elephants live in large groups in forests.
  • web/a/spider/carefully/its/weaves → A spider carefully weaves its web.
  • crocodile/waits/the/silently/riverbank/the/near → The crocodile waits silently near the riverbank.
  • a/swims/turtle/slowly/in/ocean/the → A turtle swims slowly in the ocean.
Let us Speak & Listen

A. Tongue Twisters

Practice speaking these quickly to improve pronunciation and fluency:

  1. Four fierce frogs fight for flies.
  2. Fast frogs flip-flop across fluffy fields.
  3. Funny frogs flap floppy feet.

B. Phonics: Vowel Contrast (/e/ vs /a/)

Listen and repeat pairs to distinguish short vowel sounds:

  • bet vs bat
  • set vs sat
  • men vs man
  • leg vs lag
  • met vs mat

C. Listening Activity: My Frog's World

Instructions to draw and describe a personalized frog habitat:

  1. Draw a happy setting (pond, forest, or rainy area).
  2. Draw a frog with specific features (webbed feet, colors).
  3. Decorate with descriptive words from the poem.
  4. Color the drawing creatively.
Let us Write & Explore

A. Message Writing

Message from Hopper the Frog: Hopper writes about living on a lily pad, splashing in the rain, sitting in puddles, croaking, and eating mosquitoes.

Writing Task: Write a friendly reply to Hopper sharing your own favorite hobbies and activities.

B. Did You Know? (Fun Facts)

  • Frog vs. Toad: Frogs have smooth, moist skin and long legs for hopping. Toads have dry, rough, bumpy skin and shorter legs for walking or hopping.
  • National Aquatic Animal of India: The Ganges river dolphin. It is blind/cannot see well and uses echolocation (sounds) to navigate in water.

C. Let us Explore: Aquatic Animals

Research and find facts about these fascinating aquatic creatures:

  • Puffer fish: Can inflate into a ball and some are highly poisonous.
  • Jellyfish: Gelatinous, brainless creatures that have been around for millions of years.
  • Seahorse: A type of fish where the male carries the eggs.
  • Starfish: Marine invertebrates typically with five arms and the ability to regenerate lost limbs.
  • Octopus: Highly intelligent eight-armed mollusks with camouflage abilities.
  • Catfish: Named for their prominent barbels, which resemble a cat's whiskers.
