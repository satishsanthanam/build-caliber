Class 9 — Chapter 7: Carrier of Words

Prose Summary: Khetaram, the Gramin Dak Sewak

This section explores the life of Khetaram, a dedicated Gramin Dak Sewak (GDS) serving the remote village of Somarad in the Thar Desert of Rajasthan, near the Indo-Pakistan border.

The Life and Struggles of Khetaram

  • Extreme Conditions: Khetaram walks 20 km daily across soft sand dunes in scorching summer temperatures reaching up to 50°C.
  • Physical Toll: His left shoulder is slumped from years of carrying a heavy mailbag that can weigh up to 28 kg.
  • Sole Connection: He serves as the only link between the far-flung hamlets (dhaanis) and the rest of the world, where mobile networks are virtually non-existent.

The Role of Gramin Dak Sewaks (GDS)

  • Workforce: GDS make up more than 50% of India Post's total workforce, serving inaccessible terrains like the frozen deserts of Ladakh, the islands of Lakshadweep, and riverine communities in the Northeast.
  • Financial Trust: Beyond delivering mail, GDS help rural depositors manage their monthly savings, reflecting deep-rooted public trust in the postal system.

Social Significance and Cultural Customs

  • Bearer of News: Khetaram reads letters and drafts replies for illiterate villagers.
  • Good News: News of births or weddings is celebrated, and villagers offer him a piece of jaggery.
  • Bad News: Letters bearing news of death have a torn right corner. Khetaram dreads these missives; he reads them outside the house and tears them to bits to symbolically destroy the bad news.
Poetry Analysis: "Words" by Charles Swain

A reflective poem exploring the limitations of spoken words and the supreme value of sincere, heartfelt communication.

Stanza Breakdown & Meaning

  • Stanza 1: Words alone cannot satisfy the heart; they are fleeting like "summer birds" and leave behind empty air.
  • Stanza 2: To a weary heart (a pilgrim on earth), empty words are as worthless as common weeds.
  • Stanza 3: A few sincere words spoken truly bring deeper joy than a host of meaningless words that reach only the head but never touch the heart.
  • Stanza 4: A cheerful voice that brightens a lonely home often uses the fewest words.
  • Stanza 5 & 6: Empty words may make a "gaudy show" like fruitless plants, but they fail when put to the test.

Literary Devices in "Words"

  • Simile: "words, like summer birds, depart" (comparing fleeting words to migratory birds); "words are of as little worth / As just so many weeds".
  • Metaphor: "The heart, a pilgrim upon earth" (comparing the heart to a traveler); "walking sandman" (from the prose section).
  • Hyperbole: "The world might hold a feast" (exaggerating the impact if words could truly satisfy).
Poetry Analysis: "Weigh Your Words" by E. F. Hayward

A didactic poem emphasizing the moral responsibility of speech and the lasting impact of our utterances.

Key Themes

  • Mindful Speech: We must "weigh" what we say and only speak what we truly mean to save unnecessary talk and keep our conscience clean.
  • The Danger of Idle Words: Careless words and empty promises can cause deep heartache to those who believe them.
  • Permanence of Words: Once a hurtful word is written on someone's heart, it can never be erased.

Grammar & Language Structures

Present Perfect Tense

Used to describe actions completed in the past that have a direct impact on or connection to the present.

  • Structure: Subject + has/have + Past Participle (V3)
  • Examples of Functions:
    • Recently completed action: "I have just eaten a mango."
    • Life experiences: "Have you ever seen a play before?"
    • Action completed in the past with present impact: "I have studied in this school for six years."

Active and Passive Voice

  • Active Voice: The subject performs the action.
    Example: "GDS delivers the mail at the border."
  • Passive Voice: The subject receives the action.
    Example: "The mail is delivered by the GDS at the border."
Writing & Communication Tasks

Condolence Messages

A formal expression of sympathy sent to someone experiencing grief due to the death of a loved one.

  • Key Elements: Expressing deep sadness, offering condolences, extending sympathy to the family, and praying for strength/peace for the departed soul.

The Study of Philately

Philately is the study of postage stamps and postal history. It serves as a medium to preserve and celebrate national heritage, historical figures, monuments, and festivals.
