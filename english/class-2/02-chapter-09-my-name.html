Class 2 — Chapter 9: My Name

Story Summary: My Name

Once there was a little fly who could not remember his name. He tried very hard but could not recall it. He went on a journey asking various characters to help him find his name:

  • He asked an ant, who suggested he ask the tree.
  • He asked the tree, who suggested he ask the cow sleeping in its shade.
  • He asked the cow, who suggested he ask the grass.
  • He asked the grass, who suggested he ask the fallen leaves.
  • He asked a leaf. Before the leaf could answer, the wind blew and the leaf began to fly.

Seeing the leaf fly, the fly suddenly remembered his name: "My name is Fly!"

Vocabulary & Word Study

New Words

  • little: Small in size.
  • ant: A tiny insect.
  • shade: An area sheltered from the sun.
  • dear: Loved or cherished.
  • grass: Green vegetation eaten by cows.
  • wind: Moving air.
  • remembered: Brought back to mind.

Sight Words

Practice reading these words quickly: his, do you, it, my, one, when, about.

Exercises & Activities

Let us speak

  1. How did the fly find his name? (By seeing a leaf fly in the wind).
  2. If you forget your name, whom would you ask?

Let us listen (Animal Sounds)

  • Crow: caw
  • Sparrow: chirp
  • Lion: roar
  • Cat: meow
  • Bee: buzz
  • Duck: quack

Let us write

Complete the sentences using the help box (leaf, tree, grass, cow, fly):

  • The ant said, "I don't know. You may ask the tree I climb."
  • The tree said, "I don't know. You may ask the cow sleeping in my shade."
  • The cow said, "I don't know. You may ask the grass I eat."
  • The grass said, "I don't know. You may ask the leaf fallen on me."
  • Seeing the leaf fly, the fly remembered his name.
