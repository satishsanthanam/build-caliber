Class 2 — Chapter 3: It is Fun

Poem: It is Fun

This poem celebrates the joy of movement and pretending to be different animals.

Poem Verses

It’s fun to be this,
It’s fun to be that,
To leap like a lamb,
To climb like a cat.

To hop like a frog,
To swim like a fish,
To trot like a horse,
To fly like a bird.

I can act
Just like them all,
It’s fun to be this,
It’s fun to be that.

Vocabulary & Sight Words

Sight Words: this, that, like, to, them, all, none

New Words: fun, climb, leap, swim, trot, fly

Let us Speak & Write

Speaking Activities

  • Animal Sounds: Listen to and identify sounds like the croaking of a frog, cackling of a hen, quack of a duck, and bleat of a sheep.
  • Role Play: Answer questions about what you would do as different animals (e.g., "If you were a cat, what would you do?").
  • Sentence Making: Use action words like climb, leap, swim, trot, and fly in sentences (e.g., "Monkeys climb trees.").

Writing Exercises

  • Complete sentences about animal actions: "I can [action] like a [animal]."
  • Identify animals/birds that can swim (other than fish).
  • Identify animals that live on trees.
  • Answer comprehension questions about the poem's activities.
Picture Reading

Observe the underwater scene and describe what you see. Key elements in the picture include:

  • A yellow submarine with people inside.
  • Marine life: dolphin, seahorse, octopus, jellyfish, whale, turtle, and various colorful fish.
  • A mermaid sitting on a rock.
  • A diver exploring the ocean.
Let us Think: Matching Actions

Match the animals to their characteristic movements:

  • Horse: trots
  • Lamb: leaps
  • Snake: crawls
  • Squirrel: runs
  • Monkey: swings

  • Group 3: a bin, a ring, a ship
