Class 2 — Chapter 6: Between Home and School

Story Summary: Between Home and School

This chapter follows a young boy named Ravi, a student of Class 2, and his friend as they walk to school. Instead of taking a standard path, they take the shortest and most adventurous route, filled with fun activities nature has to offer.

The Route to School

  • Passing through paddy fields.
  • Walking through a mango grove.
  • Reaching the main road to get to school.

Fun Activities on the Way

Despite doing all these playful activities, Ravi and his friend always reach school on time:

  • Jumping over canals
  • Climbing trees
  • Swinging from branches
  • Watching garden lizards
Vocabulary & New Words
  • school: A place for educating children.
  • paddy: Rice in the husk, either growing or gathered.
  • fields: Areas of open land, especially one planted with crops.
  • grove: A small wood, orchard, or group of trees (e.g., mango grove).
  • route: A way or course taken in getting from a starting point to a destination.
  • canals: Artificial waterways constructed to allow the passage of boats or to convey water.
  • branches: Parts of a tree growing out from the trunk.
  • lizards: Small, reptiles with long bodies and tails.
  • bicycle: A vehicle consisting of two wheels held in a frame one behind the other.
Exercises & Activities

Let us speak

  • What time do you leave home for school? At what time does your school start?
  • Tell your classmates about the fun you had on your way to school.

Let us write

A. Fill in the blanks with ch:

  1. Today I have brought upma for lunch.
  2. My friend Unni loves to eat banana chips.
  3. Our Teacher put up a new chart in the classroom. It is on the wall behind her chair.

B. Listening Exercise:

Listen and point to: Chart, Chalk, Chair.

C. Identification:

Circle the things Ravi and his friend see on their way to school:

  • Seen: canals, mango grove, paddy fields, lizards, main road, trees
  • Not Seen: bank, hospital, water park, park, cars, train

D. Jumbled Letters:

  • o h m e → home
  • a n c a l → canal
  • d a p y d → paddy
  • o n m g a → mango

Let us do

A. Make sentences using:

  • gate: The school gate is painted blue.
  • road: We must walk carefully on the road.
  • tree: The mango tree is full of fruits.
  • lunch: I share my lunch with my friends.
  • lizard: I saw a small lizard on the wall.

B. Vehicle Activity:

Cut out pictures of vehicles from old magazines or newspapers, paste them on a sheet, and write their names underneath.

Let us draw

Draw the route from your home to your school in your notebook.
