Class 2 — Chapter 1: My Bicycle

Poem: My Bicycle

Poem Recitation

Stanza 1:
I have a red bicycle.
I pedal and I ride.
Trin… trin… trin… goes the bell.
Makes my friends move aside.

Stanza 2:
The seat is blue in colour,
The tyres and pedals are black.
Sometimes fast, sometimes slow,
But my bicycle is always on track.

Stanza 3:
To keep my bicycle moving,
All I need is some air.
I put on my little helmet,
And go happily everywhere.

Theme & Summary

The poem describes a child's joy in riding their red bicycle. It highlights the features of the bicycle (red body, blue seat, black tyres and pedals, and a ringing bell) and emphasizes safety by mentioning wearing a helmet.

Vocabulary & Sight Words

New Words

  • pedal: A foot-operated lever used to power a bicycle.
  • aside: To one side; out of the way.
  • sometimes: Occasionally; now and then.
  • track: A path or course.
  • helmet: A protective hat worn to prevent head injury.
  • everywhere: In or to all places.

Sight Words

Practice reading these common words: I, and, is, in, my.

Let us Speak & Phonics

Discussion Questions

  • Have you seen a bicycle? Where have you seen it?
  • What colour is it? Who generally rides it?
  • Have you seen a tricycle? Have you seen a wheelchair?

Phonics: Vowel Sound 'a'

This section introduces the short vowel sound 'a' (as in 'pan'). Notice the use of an before words starting with 'a':

  • Group 1 (starts with 'a'): an ant, an axe, an apple
  • Group 2: a rat, a cat, a mat
  • Group 3: a pan, a man, a cap
Let us Write & Exercises

Parts of a Bicycle

Identify and label the parts of a bicycle using these words:

  • seat
  • pedal
  • wheel
  • bell
  • handle
  • carrier
  • brake

Sentence Completion

  1. The child in the poem has a red bicycle.
  2. The sound of the bell makes people move aside.
  3. The tyres and pedals are black.

True or False

  1. The child has a blue bicycle. (False / ✗)
  2. The bell goes trin... trin... trin. (True / ✓)
  3. The child’s bicycle is always fast. (False / ✗)
  4. The child needs some water to keep the bicycle moving. (False / ✗)
  5. The child puts on a little helmet while riding the bicycle. (True / ✓)
