Class 2 — Chapter 2: Picture Reading (Out! Out!)

Chapter Overview & Picture Reading

This chapter introduces young learners to visual comprehension, vocabulary building, and reading through interactive illustrations and an engaging story about childhood games.

Picture Reading Questions

  • What do you see in the picture?
  • How many children are there in the picture?
  • What are the children doing?
  • How are children having fun?
  • What games do you play?
Story Summary: "OUT! OUT!"

The story follows two children, Jeet and Babli, playing together on a holiday.

Key Plot Points

  • Holiday Fun: Jeet and Babli play multiple games, including skipping rope and hide-and-seek.
  • The Lost Ball: They decide to play 'bat and ball'. Jeet throws the ball, and Babli hits it. The ball flies into Mohit's locked garden and is lost, causing the game to stop.
  • Creative Solution: Babli knows how to make a homemade ball. She gathers rags, paper, wool, and string to roll and tie a new ball together.
  • The Exciting Finish: The game resumes. Babli throws the new ball, and Jeet hits it so hard that the ball bursts and scatters in the air. Babli catches a flying piece of rag and happily shouts, "OUT! OUT!"
Vocabulary & Sight Words

New Words

  • skipped: Jumped over a rope repeatedly.
  • threw: Propelled something through the air with force.
  • courtyard: An open area of ground surrounded by walls or buildings.
  • gathered: Brought together from different places.
  • brought: Took or carried along to a place.
  • picked: Chose or took up.

Sight Words

Practice reading these common words: This, a, how, also, they, after, and, of.

Phonics & Grammar

Vowel Sound 'e'

This section introduces the short vowel sound 'e' as in pen. Notice the use of the article an before words starting with the vowel 'e'.

  • Using 'an': an egg, an engine, an elephant
  • Using 'a': a pen, a pencil, a bell, a kettle, a hen, a well

Opposites

  • up × down
  • in × out
  • open × close
  • fat × thin
  • hot × cold
  • small × big

Joining Sentences with 'and'

Learn how to combine two sentences using the conjunction and.

  • Example 1: "Suresh went to school." + "Gopal went to school." → Suresh and Gopal went to school.
  • Example 2: "I like to eat mangoes." + "I like to eat grapes." → I like to eat mangoes and grapes.
  • Example 3: "The cat sat on the mat." + "The dog sat on the mat." → The cat and the dog sat on the mat.
