Class 3 — Chapter 11: Chanda Mama Counts the Stars

Story Summary & Analysis

The story follows Chanda Mama (the Moon), who changes shape from a perfect circle to other forms. He attempts to count all the stars in the night sky but repeatedly fails because he falls asleep, forgets his count by morning, or gets interrupted by the sunrise. Upset by his inability to complete the task, he cries. The Sun comforts him by explaining that the number of stars in the sky is equal to the number of lovely children on Earth. This realization makes Chanda Mama happy, and he now smiles down at both the stars and the children every night.

Key Themes

  • Wonder and Curiosity: The natural urge to explore and count the wonders of the night sky.
  • Comfort and Friendship: The Sun acts as a comforting friend to the Moon, offering a beautiful perspective to resolve his sadness.
  • Connection to Children: Linking the infinite beauty of the stars to the joy and presence of children on Earth.
Vocabulary & Key Words
  • Round: Shaped like a circle or sphere.
  • Count: To determine the total number of items.
  • Tears: Drops of clear salty liquid secreted from the eyes, typically when crying.
Grammar & Language Study: Here and There

We use here to tell the location of things that are near us. Similarly, there is used to tell the location of things that are far from us.

Examples

  • We are here on the Earth. The Moon is there in the sky.
  • I have a sharpener here in my pencil box.
  • The crayons are there in the big box.

Practice Exercises

  • The students are playing kabaddi there.
  • __________ is your shirt.
  • __________ is my bicycle.
  • I went to Chandigarh last year. My brother lives __________.
  • This is my bench. I sit __________ with my friends.
Exercises & Activities

Comprehension Questions

  1. Does the Moon look the same every night?
  2. What did Chanda Mama try to count?
  3. Why did Chanda Mama fall asleep?
  4. Who helped Chanda Mama?
  5. Complete the sentence: The number of stars in the sky is the same as ___________________.

Think and Say

  • I call the Moon, Chanda Mama. What do you call him in your language?
  • Chanda Mama was not able to count the stars. What things can you not count?
  • The Moon is visible during the night. What can you see in the night sky?

Categorization: Countable vs. Uncountable

Sort the following items into two categories: Can be counted easily or Too many to be counted:

  • Children in the classroom
  • Flowers in a garden
  • Books in the library
  • Pencils in your pencil box
  • Fingers on your right hand
  • Children in your city or town
  • Pages in your English notebook
  • The hairs on your head

Project Work: Wind Chimes

Create a wind chime using cardboard, coloured paper, scissors, marker, thread, and glue. Cut out shapes of the crescent moon, stars, and sun, decorate them, and attach them together with thread.
