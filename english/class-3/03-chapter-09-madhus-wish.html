Class 3 — Chapter 9: Madhu's Wish

Story Summary

Madhu's Wish is a story written by Sudha Murty about a boy named Madhu who loved food and learned a valuable lesson about the importance of water.

Plot Outline

  • Madhu loved eating and sat under a banyan tree daily to eat his lunch.
  • One day, he shared his food with a hungry old man.
  • In return, the old man granted him a wish from the banyan tree.
  • Madhu wished for different types of food and nothing else, ignoring the tree's warning to think it over.
  • He enjoyed laddoos, fruits, and savouries, but soon got hiccups.
  • With no water available, he tried drinking sweet drinks like sharbat, kheer, and badam milk, but his hiccups did not stop.
  • Desperate, Madhu realized that water is irreplaceable and wished for all the food to disappear in exchange for water.

Characters

  • Madhu: A kind-hearted boy who loves food but acts greedily without thinking of his basic needs.
  • The Old Man: A kind and generous person who rewards Madhu's kindness with a magical wish.
  • The Banyan Tree: A wise, magical tree that grants Madhu's wishes and teaches him a lesson.

Moral of the Story

No other drink can replace the importance of simple water. We must not let greed blind us to our basic necessities.

Vocabulary & Word Meanings
  • forever: For all time; always.
  • immediately: Right away; without delay.
  • appeared: Came into sight.
  • savouries: Salty and spicy food items.
  • hiccups: Involuntary sounds made in the throat.
  • disappear: To go out of sight; vanish.
  • instead: In place of something else.
  • replace: To take the place of.
  • thirsty: Feeling a need to drink water.
Grammar & Language Study

Describing Words (Qualities)

Words used to describe the character or nature of people:

  • kind: Caring and sharing with others (e.g., Madhu sharing food).
  • greedy: Wanting everything for oneself (e.g., Madhu wanting only food).
  • generous: Giving freely to others (e.g., The old man giving a special gift).
  • selfish: Not sharing with anyone.
  • brave: Showing courage in danger.
  • lazy: Doing nothing and lying in bed.
  • polite: Wishing everyone and showing good manners.

Conjunctions: 'and' & 'but'

Conjunctions are joining words used to connect sentences.

  • Using and: Used to join similar ideas or items.
    Example: "Aman has a pen, a pencil and an eraser."
  • Using but: Used to join contrasting or opposing ideas.
    Example: "Sam works in the afternoon but Pratham works at night."

Expressing Reason with 'because'

The word because is used to link an action with its reason or cause.

  • Example: Madhu shared his food with the old man because he felt sorry for him.
  • Example: Madhu asked for water because he got hiccups and was thirsty.
Activities & Exercises

Food Categorization

Sorting food items into correct groups:

  • Fruits: banana, melon, guava, jackfruit
  • Sweets: kheer, laddoo
  • Salty snacks: kachori, potato chips, vada, samosa

Fun with Words: Word Pyramids

Creating word pyramids where every word starts with the same letter:

  • C-Pyramid: Carrots → Cut Carrots → Chandu Cut Carrots
  • A-Pyramid: Apples → Ate Apples → Anu Ate Apples
