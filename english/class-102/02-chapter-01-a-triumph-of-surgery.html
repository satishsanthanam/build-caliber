Class 102 — Chapter 1: A Triumph of Surgery

Chapter Overview

"A Triumph of Surgery" by James Herriot is a heartwarming story about a small dog named Tricki, who is pampered and overfed by his wealthy mistress, Mrs. Pumphrey. The dog falls seriously ill due to overeating and lack of exercise. The narrator, Mr. James Herriot, a tactful veterinary surgeon, cures Tricki not through complex medical surgeries, but through a simple, natural regime of strict diet and physical activity.

Detailed Plot Summary

Tricki's Deteriorating Health

Mr. Herriot is shocked to see Tricki in the street. The dog has become hugely fat, resembling a "bloated sausage with a leg at each corner." His eyes are bloodshot and rheumy, and his tongue lolls from his jaws. Mrs. Pumphrey explains that she thought Tricki was suffering from malnutrition, so she has been giving him extra food between meals, including malt, cod-liver oil, Horlicks, cream cakes, and chocolates. Despite Herriot's warnings to cut down on sweets and increase exercise, Mrs. Pumphrey's indulgence continues.

The Call for Help & Hospitalization

As predicted, Tricki's health worsens. He refuses to eat his favorite dishes, suffers from bouts of vomiting, and lies panting on a rug. Mr. Herriot realizes that the only way to save Tricki is to remove him from Mrs. Pumphrey's house. He suggests hospitalizing Tricki for a fortnight under observation. Although Mrs. Pumphrey is distraught and nearly faints, Herriot takes a firm line and carries the wrapped dog to his car amidst the wailings of the household staff.

Treatment at the Surgery

At the veterinary surgery, Tricki is initially listless and ignored by the other dogs. Herriot treats him using a natural approach:

  • Days 1-2: No food is given, only plenty of water.
  • Day 3: Tricki begins to show interest in his surroundings and whimpers upon hearing other dogs.
  • Subsequent Days: He joins the other dogs (like Joe the greyhound) in their friendly scrimmages, runs about, and fights for his share of food during mealtimes. He recovers rapidly without any medicinal treatment.

The Luxurious Convalescence

When Mrs. Pumphrey hears that Tricki is convalescing, she starts sending fresh eggs (two dozen at a time), bottles of wine, and brandy to enrich his blood. Instead of giving these to Tricki, Mr. Herriot and his partners enjoy them as luxurious additions to their daily meals. This tempts Herriot to keep Tricki as a permanent guest, but knowing Mrs. Pumphrey's distress, he calls her after a fortnight to collect the dog.

The Reunion

Mrs. Pumphrey arrives in a chauffeur-driven car. Tricki, now a lithe, hard-muscled animal, leaps from Herriot's arms into his mistress's lap, licking her face and barking with joy. Overwhelmed with gratitude, Mrs. Pumphrey declares with tears in her eyes, "Oh, Mr Herriot, how can I ever thank you? This is a triumph of surgery!"

Character Analysis

Mr. James Herriot

  • Profession: Veterinary Surgeon and Narrator.
  • Traits: Highly practical, tactful, full of common sense, and compassionate.
  • Role: He understands that Tricki's illness is psychological and behavioral rather than clinical. He manages both the over-indulgent owner and the overfed pet with great diplomacy.

Mrs. Pumphrey

  • Status: Extremely wealthy and lonely lady.
  • Traits: Foolishly indulgent, over-sensitive, and deeply attached to her pet.
  • Role: Her excessive love and pampering harm Tricki. She represents people who mistake indulgence for care.

Tricki

  • Identity: A small, pampered dog.
  • Traits: Greedy but sweet-tempered; never refuses food.
  • Transformation: Changes from a bloated, listless animal into an active, lithe, and hard-muscled dog when placed in a natural, competitive environment.
Glossary & Vocabulary
  • sausage: Finely minced meat stuffed into long cylindrical cases.
  • rheumy: A watery discharge from a mucous membrane, especially of the nose or eyes.
  • listless: Lacking energy and enthusiasm.
  • lumbago: Muscular pain in the lower part of the back.
  • regime: A prescribed course of exercise and diet.
  • distraught: Extremely worried and upset.
  • surgery: A place where a doctor, dentist, or veterinary surgeon treats patients.
  • scrimmage: A rough or confused struggle or fight.
  • convalescing: Recovering health and strength after illness.
  • lithe: Thin, supple, and graceful; flexible.
Key Questions & Discussion

Think About It

  1. What kind of a person do you think the narrator, a veterinary surgeon, is? Would you say he is tactful as well as full of common sense?

    Yes, Mr. Herriot is extremely tactful and full of common sense. He immediately diagnoses Tricki's problem as overfeeding and prescribes a simple cure of diet and exercise rather than unnecessary medical procedures. He also tactfully handles Mrs. Pumphrey's emotional outbursts and enjoys the treats she sends without harming the dog.

  2. Do you think Tricki was happy to go home? What do you think will happen now?

    Tricki was extremely happy to see his mistress, as shown by his tremendous leap into her lap. However, if Mrs. Pumphrey resumes her indulgent ways and overfeeds him again, Tricki might fall ill once more.

  3. Do you think this is a real-life episode, or mere fiction? Or is it a mixture of both?

    It is likely a mixture of both. James Herriot was a real-life veterinary surgeon, and many of his stories are based on his actual experiences, though some elements may be slightly exaggerated for comic and narrative effect.

Talk About It

  • Silly behavior vs. Harm: Mrs. Pumphrey's foolish indulgence, though stemming from love, causes real physical harm to Tricki. This highlights how blind love and lack of discipline can be destructive.
  • Parents like Mrs. Pumphrey: Many parents spoil their children with excessive material comforts and lack of discipline, which can negatively affect their physical and mental growth.
