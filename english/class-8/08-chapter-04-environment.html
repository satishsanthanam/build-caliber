Class 8 — Chapter 4: Unit 4: Environment

Unit Overview: Environment

Unit 4 of the Class 8 English textbook "Poorvi" focuses on the theme of Environment. Through two prose pieces and one poem, it explores humanity's deep connection with nature, the resilience of life, the cycle of agriculture, and the importance of letting the Earth rest.

1. The Cherry Tree (Prose) - Ruskin Bond

Plot Summary

Six-year-old Rakesh lives with his grandfather on the outskirts of Mussoorie. One day, he buys cherries and plants the last seed in a soft corner of the garden. Over the years, the seed sprouts and faces numerous threats: a goat eats its leaves, a grass-cutter cuts it in half, and heavy winter snow bends it. Despite these challenges, the cherry tree survives and grows. By the time Rakesh is nine, the tree blossoms and bears its first cherries. The story highlights the resilience of nature and the joy of nurturing life.

Characters

  • Rakesh: A young, caring boy who plants the cherry seed and watches it grow with dedication and patience.
  • Grandfather: A wise old man who guides Rakesh, telling him stories and teaching him how to care for the plant.

Vocabulary & Word Meanings

  • stunted: Stopped from growing to the usual size.
  • outskirts: The outer parts of a town or city.
  • tang: A strong, sharp taste.
  • yielding: Soft and giving way under pressure.
  • haunches: Sitting on the feet with legs bent.
  • peered down: Looked carefully or with difficulty.
  • plodded: Walked slowly with heavy steps.
  • scrambled: Moved quickly and with difficulty.
  • scythe: A tool with a long, curved blade for cutting grass.
  • foliage: Green leaves of a plant or tree.
  • grumpy: Bad-tempered or irritable.
  • gazed: Looked steadily with admiration or wonder.
  • flitted: Flew or moved quickly and lightly.
  • reclining: Leaning or lying back in a relaxed position.
2. Harvest Hymn (Poem) - Sarojini Naidu

Poem Structure & Voices

  • Men's Voices: Praise Surya (the Sun God) for providing warmth and light, and Varuna (the Rain God) for providing water and nourishment to the crops.
  • Women's Voices: Praise Prithvi (Mother Earth) for her fertile soil and plentiful bosom that feeds humanity.
  • All Voices (Chorus): Praise Brahma (the Creator/Lord of the Universe) as the ultimate source of life, the seed, and the harvest.

Archaic Words & Meanings

  • thou: You (subject)
  • thee: You (object)
  • thy: Your
  • thine: Yours
  • art: Are

Poetic Devices & Symbolism

  • Imagery: "The gold of our fields and the gold of our fruit" (visual imagery of a rich harvest).
  • Alliteration: "We bring thee our lives and our labours for tribute" (repetition of the 'l' sound).
  • Metaphor: "Thou art the Seed and the Scythe of our harvests" (comparing the divine to the beginning and end of life).
  • Personification: "Sweet and omnipotent mother, O Earth!" (treating the Earth as a mother).
3. Waiting for the Rain (Prose) - Kamakshi Balasubramanian

Plot Summary

Velu, a hardworking farmer, is devastated by a severe drought that leaves his fields dry, cracked, and barren. Frustrated and dejected, he seeks answers from the weather office but finds no hope. While resting under a large tree, he meets an old woman. She gently explains that the drought is Nature's way of letting the Earth rest after years of continuous cultivation. Velu realizes the wisdom in her words. Soon after, a cool breeze blows, clouds gather, and it begins to rain, bringing joy back to Velu.

Key Concept: Fallowing

Fallowing is a sustainable land management practice where agricultural land is left unsown for one or more vegetative cycles. This allows the soil to recover its nutrients, retain moisture, and control pests naturally.

Vocabulary & Word Meanings

  • crimson: Deep red colour.
  • crusted: Formed a hard outer layer.
  • forlorn: Sad and neglected.
  • consultations: Discussions or meetings for advice.
  • dejected: Sad and disappointed.
  • bitterly: Hurtfully or with anger.
  • ceaseless: Continuous and non-stopping.
4. Grammar & Language Skills

Tenses (Past Forms)

  • Simple Past Tense: Used for completed actions in the past. Example: "Rakesh bought a bunch of cherries."
  • Past Progressive Tense: Used for ongoing actions in the past. Example: "Rakesh was looking for Grandfather."
  • Past Perfect Tense: Used for an action completed before another action in the past. Example: "The tree had grown another foot before he returned."

Determiners

Determiners are words placed before nouns to clarify what the noun refers to.

  • Articles: a, an, the
  • Demonstrative: this, that, these, those
  • Possessive: my, our, your, his, her, its, their
  • Definite Numeral: one, two, three, etc.
  • Indefinite Numeral: some, any, no, all, much, many, few, several, little
  • Distributive: each, every, either, neither

Homophones

Words that sound the same but have different spellings and meanings.

  • weather (atmospheric conditions) vs. whether (expressing a doubt or choice)
  • piece (a portion) vs. peace (tranquility)
  • plain (flat land) vs. plane (airplane/flat surface)
  • rain (precipitation) vs. reign (rule of a monarch)
