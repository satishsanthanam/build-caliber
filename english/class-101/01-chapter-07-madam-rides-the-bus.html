Class 101 — Chapter 7: Madam Rides the Bus

Madam Rides the Bus - Prose Summary

Introduction & Valli's Deepest Desire

The story revolves around Valliammai (Valli), an eight-year-old girl who is highly curious and has no playmates. Her favorite pastime is standing in her doorway to watch the street. Her deepest desire is to ride the bus that travels between her village and the nearest town.

Planning and Calculation

Valli meticulously plans her journey by listening to neighbors' conversations and asking discreet questions. She learns key details:

  • The town is six miles away.
  • The fare is thirty paise one way.
  • The trip takes forty-five minutes.
  • She saves sixty paise by resisting temptations like buying toys, peppermints, and riding the merry-go-round (thriftily saving).

The Journey to Town

Valli slips out during her mother's afternoon nap. On the bus, her self-respect and confidence shine as she refuses help from the conductor, who jokingly calls her madam. She is mesmerized by the outside views: green fields, palm trees, and a young cow running in front of the bus, which makes her laugh heartily.

The Return Journey & Mystery of Death

In town, Valli stays on the bus to return. On the way back, she sees the same cow lying dead by the roadside, struck by a vehicle. This sight fills her with deep sadness, dampening her enthusiasm. She experiences her first real encounter with the mystery of life and death.

Character Analysis

Valli (Valliammai)

  • Independent & Self-Respecting: Refuses help to climb the bus and declines a free drink from the conductor.
  • Meticulous Planner: Calculates fares, timings, and saves money systematically.
  • Sensitive & Observant: Deeply affected by the beauty of nature and the tragedy of the dead cow.

The Bus Conductor

  • Jolly & Good-Natured: Fond of joking, calls Valli 'madam' to match her proud demeanor, and offers to buy her a cold drink.
Prose Vocabulary & Glossary

The Tale of Custard the Dragon - Poem Summary

Poem Overview & Characters

A humorous ballad by Ogden Nash about Belinda and her pets who boast of their bravery, while Custard the dragon is mocked as a coward.

  • Belinda: Brave as a barrel full of bears.
  • Ink (Black Kitten) & Blink (Grey Mouse): Chase lions down the stairs.
  • Mustard (Yellow Dog): Brave as a tiger in a rage.
  • Custard (Dragon): Cries for a nice safe cage, despite having sharp teeth, spikes, and a fiery mouth.

The Pirate Encounter

When a pirate breaks in, everyone flees in terror except Custard. Custard snorts like an engine, clashes his tail, and gobbles the pirate up. Afterward, the others make excuses and claim they would have been braver, which Custard humbly accepts.
