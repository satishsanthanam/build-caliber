Class 10 (Words and Expressions) — Chapter 7: Madam Rides the Bus

Let's Begin

An introductory discussion based on the story 'Madam Rides the Bus', focusing on Valli's determination, planning, and savings to fulfill her desire of taking a bus ride.

Key Discussion Points
  • Valli's self-control in saving money by resisting temptations like peppermints, toys, and balloons.
  • The exciting sights Valli observed from the bus during her journey.
  • The contrast in her emotions and excitement between the onward and return journeys.
  • Personal travel experiences using various modes of transport (bicycle, boat, bus, train, etc.).
Reading Comprehension

Text I: Pedalling through the Golden Years

An inspiring account of Vishav Driman (65) and her husband Kamal Jeet (69), who embarked on a post-retirement cycling adventure to discover the hidden treasures of Uttarakhand.

  • The Journey: Started with short daily rides, which gradually extended into long-distance cycling expeditions and participation in cyclotrons.
  • The Route: A challenging 100km trail starting from Ghanta Ghar (Dehradun) through picturesque locations like Kimadi, Hathi Paon, Mussoorie, and Chaar Dukaan, ending at George Everest Point.
  • Key Challenges: High altitude, steep slopes, sharp curves, cold winds, and physically strenuous activity at an advanced age.

Vocabulary to Understand:

  • acclimatised: Get used to a new climate or conditions.
  • cyclotrons: Cycling events or circular cycling tracks/races.
  • strenuous: Requiring great effort or exertion.
  • venture: Undertake a risky or daring journey.
  • inexplicable: Unable to be explained or accounted for.
  • picturesque: Visually attractive, especially in a quaint or charming way.

Text II: Going Down Hill on a Bicycle, A Boy's Song

A poem by Henry Charles Beeching capturing the sheer joy, thrill, and sense of freedom experienced while cycling downhill.

Poetic Themes:

  • The sensation of flying and sharing a "golden moment" of freedom with birds.
  • The contrast between the effortless, swift descent and the laborious climb up the hill.
  • The philosophical message: Hard work and toil (climbing with toil) eventually reward a person with wings of joy and satisfaction.

Vocabulary to Understand:

  • poised: Balanced and ready for action.
  • heedful: Mindful, attentive, and careful.
  • slackens: Slows down or becomes less active.
  • treadles: Pedals of the bicycle.
  • vale: A valley.
Vocabulary & Grammar

Vocabulary Exercises

Finding words from the text 'Madam Rides the Bus' matching specific meanings:

  • inquisitive: Curious / eager to know.
  • intricate: Very detailed or complicated.
  • mesmerizing: Captivating / spellbinding.
  • immense: Extremely large or great.
  • hardly: Scarcely / barely.

Grammar: Phrasal Verbs

Understanding Phrasal Verbs (combinations of a verb and a preposition/adverb that create a new meaning).

  • Examples from text: picked up, get on.
  • Practice Verbs: break, drive, get, fill, wake, turn combined with out, down, off, on, in, up.
Editing & Listening

Editing Exercise

Correcting punctuation, capitalization, and spelling errors in a passage about the Sundarbans (e.g., correcting "sundarbans" to "Sundarbans", "unesco" to "UNESCO", "singl" to "single", "reserv" to "reserve", "Ttger" to "Tiger", "crusial" to "crucial").

Listening: Mizoram and its People

A descriptive passage highlighting the natural beauty, geography, and vibrant culture of Mizoram.

  • Etymology: Mi means human beings, Zo means hill. Hence, Mizo means hill dwellers, and Mizoram means the land of the hill dwellers.
  • Festivals: Mim Kut, Chapchar Kut, and Pawl Kut, which are closely associated with agricultural activities.
  • Mizo Dances: Community-driven, folk-based expressions of a carefree spirit, meant for participation rather than stage performance.
Speaking & Communicative Tasks

Dialogue Conversion & Role Play

Converting narrative prose from 'Madam Rides the Bus' into dramatic dialogues, focusing on tone indicators like commandingly, haughtily, loudly, smilingly, irritably, and curtly.

Communicative Task I: Offering, Accepting, and Refusing Help

Practicing conversational English for offering assistance, accepting it gracefully, or politely declining it.

Communicative Task II: Indirect Questions & Question Tags

Using conversational structures to ask for information indirectly and using question tags (e.g., "It's cold today, isn't it?") to seek agreement or confirmation.

Writing & Project Work

Writing Tasks
  • Child Safety & Freedom: Discussing whether children of Valli's age should travel alone, balancing their need for independence with safety concerns.
  • Stray Animals on Roads: Writing a paragraph on the hazards of letting domestic animals roam freely on busy roads, inspired by the tragic death of the young cow in the story.

Project Work
  • Creating travel checklists for different modes of transport (train, bus, boat, motorbike, trekking).
  • Analyzing travel tickets (bus, train, flight) to extract key details like PNR, departure/arrival times, seat numbers, and fare.
  • Writing an essay on the importance and civic duty of traveling with a valid ticket.
