Class 10 (Words and Expressions) — Chapter 2: Nelson Mandela: Long Walk to Freedom

Let's Begin & Reading Comprehension

Let's Begin

Reflecting on Nelson Mandela's struggle in South Africa, this section explores the concept that freedom comes with responsibilities. Students are encouraged to discuss how freedom and responsibilities go hand in hand.

Text I: Sardar Patel's Speech

A convocation address delivered by Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, calling upon the youth of India to build character and maintain discipline in the newly independent nation.

  • Key Themes: Hard-won freedom, individual and collective responsibility, character building, and national unity.
  • The Challenge of Freedom: Patel warns that winning freedom is only the beginning; consolidating and preserving it requires immense sacrifice, truth, and non-violence.
  • Role of Universities: Universities must train young minds to shoulder the heavy administrative and social responsibilities of the nation.
Vocabulary & Word Meanings

Look Up and Understand

  • conferring: Bestowing or presenting an honor, gift, or right.
  • endeavour: An earnest effort or attempt to achieve a goal.
  • obligation: A duty or commitment.
  • precincts: The area within the walls or boundaries of a place (e.g., alma mater).
  • unassailable: Unable to be attacked, questioned, or defeated.
  • comity of nations: The mutual courtesy and respect between nations.
  • communalism: Strong allegiance to one's own ethnic or religious community rather than to society as a whole.
  • inheritance: Legacy or assets passed down from ancestors or departed leaders.
  • resurgence: An increase or revival after a period of little activity or dullness.
